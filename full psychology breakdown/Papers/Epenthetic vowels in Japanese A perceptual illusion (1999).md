# Epenthetic vowels in Japanese: A perceptual illusion?

**Authors:** Emmanuel Dupoux, Jacques Mehler
**Year:** 1999
**Citations:** 533
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0096-1523.25.6.1568

## Abstract

In four cross-linguistic experiments comparing French and Japanese hearers, we found that the phonotactic properties of Japanese (very reduced set of syllable types) induce Japanese listeners to perceive ``illusory'' vowels inside consonant clusters in VCCV stimuli. In Experiments 1 and 2, we used a continuum of stimuli ranging from no vowel (e.g. ebzo) to a full vowel between the consonants (e.g. ebuzo). Japanese, but not French participants, reported the presence of a vowel [u] between consonants, even in stimuli with no vowel.
