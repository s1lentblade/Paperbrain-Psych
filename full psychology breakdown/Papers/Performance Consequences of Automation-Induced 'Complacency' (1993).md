**Citations:** 852

## Abstract

Abstract The effect of variations in the reliability of an automated monitoring system on human operator detection of automation failures was examined in two experiments. For four 30-min sessions, 40 subjects performed an IBM PC-based flight simulation that included manual tracking and fuel-management tasks, as well as a system-monitoring task that was under automation control. Automation reliability-the percentage of system malfunctions detected by the automation routine-either remained constant at a low or high level over time or alternated every 10 min from low to high.
