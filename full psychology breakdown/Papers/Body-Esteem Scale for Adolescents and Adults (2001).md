# Body-Esteem Scale for Adolescents and Adults

**Authors:** Beverley K. Mendelson, Donna R. White
**Year:** 2001
**Citations:** 986
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1207/s15327752jpa7601_6

## Abstract

Body esteem (BE) refers to self-evaluations of one's body or appearance. This article outlines a BE questionnaire for adolescents and adults that has 3 subscales: BE-Appearance (general feelings about appearance), BE-Weight (weight satisfaction), and BE-Attribution (evaluations attributed to others about one's body and appearance). The subscales have high internal consistency and 3-month test-retest reliability. Females scored lower than males on BE-Weight and BE-Appearance. BE-Weight was the only subscale uniquely related to weight, especially in females, with heavy individuals tending to be dissatisfied with their weight.
