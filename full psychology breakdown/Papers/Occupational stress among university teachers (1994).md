# Occupational stress among university teachers

**Authors:** Arlene Gray Blix, Glen Blix
**Year:** 1994
**Citations:** 340
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/0013188940360205

## Abstract

Summary The Person‐Environment Fit model was used to analyse the lack of fit (misfit) between motivational style and job rewards as a contributing factor in developing occupational stress symptoms in university teachers. Three motivational styles and corresponding job rewards were measured using instruments derived from Porter's motivational theory in a questionnaire mailed to 400 randomly selected tenure‐track university teachers. Occupational stress symptoms were measured by items reflecting burnout, stress‐related health problems, perceived work stress, productivity, job satisfaction and consideration for job change.
