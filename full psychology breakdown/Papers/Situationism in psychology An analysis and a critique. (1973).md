# Situationism in psychology: An analysis and a critique.

**Authors:** Kenneth S. Bowers
**Year:** 1973
**Citations:** 1,098
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/h0035592

## Abstract

This article critically analyzes the current tendency to account for human behavior largely in terms of the situation in which it occurs. This trend in effect substitutes a more or less behavioristic account of personality for a severely taxed trait conception. Although it is undoubtedly true that behavior is more situation specific than trait theory acknowledged, it is herein argued that situations are more person specific than is commonly recognized. The present article details metaphysical, psychological, and methodological assumptions and biases of situationism which have rendered it inattentive to the importance of the person in personality research.
