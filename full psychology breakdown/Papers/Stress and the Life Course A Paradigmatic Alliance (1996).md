# Stress and the Life Course: A Paradigmatic Alliance

**Authors:** Leonard I. Pearlin, Marilyn M. Skaff
**Year:** 1996
**Citations:** 444

## Abstract

Research both into the stress process and into the life course is concerned with changing lives. Yet, the conceptual paradigms that guide the work of these two fields are largely segregated, borrowing little from each other. This article explores some of the junctures at which the study of social stress might benefit from life-course perspectives and, conversely, those at which life-course research might profitably employ the vantage points of stress research.
