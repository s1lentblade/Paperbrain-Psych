# Nostalgia for early experience as a determinant of consumer preferences

**Authors:** Robert M. Schindler, Morris B. Holbrook
**Year:** 2003
**Citations:** 331
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/mar.10074

## Abstract

Abstract Studies of music, motion pictures, movie stars, and fashion products have shown that styles popular during a consumer's youth can influence the consumer's lifelong preferences. The authors present an integrative model of this phenomenon and propose that these nostalgic effects are not limited to products that relate to the arts and entertainment or are primarily aesthetic. As an illustrative example, the authors investigate the effects of early experience on consumer preferences for automobile styles.
