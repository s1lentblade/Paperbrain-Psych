## Abstract

Recent investigations suggest that emotion regulation can be conceptualized as a transdiagnosticprocess (Kring &amp; Sloan, 2010). Specifically, the habitual use of putatively maladaptiveemotion regulation strategies (e.g., rumination, suppression), and the infrequent use of putativelyadaptive strategies (e.g., acceptance, reappraisal) have been shown to predict various symptoms ofpsychopathology (Aldao et al., 2010). However, little is known about the extent to which the differentfacets that constitute the process of implementing such strategies can be conceptualized astransdiagnostic.
