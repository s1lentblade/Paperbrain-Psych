# Development of word reading fluency and spelling in a consistent orthography: An 8-year follow-up.

**Authors:** Karin Landerl, Heinz Wimmer
**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 718
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-0663.100.1.150

## Abstract

In a longitudinal study, development of word reading fluency and spelling were followed for almost 8 years. In a group of 115 students (65 girls, 50 boys) acquiring the phonologically transparent German orthography, prediction measures (letter knowledge, phonological short-term memory, phonological awareness, rapid automatized naming, and nonverbal IQ) were assessed at the beginning of Grade 1; reading fluency and spelling were tested at the end of Grade 1 as well as in Grades 4 and 8. Reading accuracy was close to ceiling in all reading assessments, such that reading fluency was not heavily influenced by differences in reading accuracy.
