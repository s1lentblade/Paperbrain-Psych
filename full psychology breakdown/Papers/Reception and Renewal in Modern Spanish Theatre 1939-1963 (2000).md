The author's primary goal is to show how foreign theater was perceived in Spain in the period circumscribed by Franco's rise to power and Spain's social, economic, and cultural rebirth in the 1960s. Reception is measured by the critical reaction to key premieres of European and North American plays and by the influence of foreign theater on Spanish playwrights.
