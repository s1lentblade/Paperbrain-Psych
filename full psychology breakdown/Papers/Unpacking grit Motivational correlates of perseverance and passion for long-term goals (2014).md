## Abstract

In two cross-sectional studies, we explored the motivational orientations correlates of the character strength of grit and its two component facets: perseverance of effort and consistency of interests over time. Specifically, we examined how individual differences in grit are explained by distinct approaches to pursuing happiness in life: <i>pleasure</i> in immediately hedonically positive activities, <i>meaning</i> in activities that serve a higher, altruistic purpose, and <i>engagement</i> in attention-absorbing activities.
