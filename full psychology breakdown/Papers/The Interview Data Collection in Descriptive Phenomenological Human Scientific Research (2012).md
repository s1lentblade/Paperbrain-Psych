# The Interview: Data Collection in Descriptive Phenomenological Human Scientific Research

**Authors:** Magnus Englander
**Year:** 2012
**Citations:** 675
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1163/156916212x632943

## Abstract

Abstract In this article, interviewing from a descriptive, phenomenological, human scientific perspective is examined. Methodological issues are raised in relation to evaluative criteria as well as reflective matters that concern the phenomenological researcher. The data collection issues covered are 1) the selection of participants, 2) the number of participants in a study, 3) the interviewer and the questions, and 4) data collection procedures. Certain conclusions were drawn indicating that phenomenological research methods cannot be evaluated on the basis of an empiricist theory of science, but must be critiqued from within a phenomenological theory of science.
