# Keys to solution in brief therapy

**Authors:** Steve de Shazer
**Year:** 1985
**Citations:** 1,048

## Abstract

Join de Shazer behind the on-way mirror for a fascinating journey into the land of brief therapy, where the emphasis is not on how problems arise, but on how to solve them. His case examples read like well-written detective novels, and his concept of 'skeleton key' interventions is both provocative and promising. This is a book that is firmly grounded in the tradition of Milton Erickson, but that extends Erickson's work into new areas. Highly recommended. -Bill O'Hanlon, Editor, Milton H.Erickson Foundation Newsletter De Shazer's work is testimony to simplicity and parsimony in the therapeutic art of addressing the complex.
