# Ethnicity and body dissatisfaction among women in the United States: A meta-analysis.

**Authors:** Shelly Grabe, Janet Shibley Hyde
**Year:** 2006
**Citations:** 653
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.132.4.622

## Abstract

The prevailing view in popular culture and the psychological literature is that White women have greater body dissatisfaction than women of color. In this meta-analysis, 6 main effect sizes were obtained for differences among Asian American, Black, Hispanic, and White women with a sample of 98 studies, yielding 222 effect sizes. The average d for the White-Black comparison was 0.29, indicating that White women are more dissatisfied, but the difference is small. All other comparisons were smaller, and many were close to zero.
