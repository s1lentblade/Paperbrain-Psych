# Making the invisible visible: Fear and disclosure of sexual orientation at work.

**Authors:** Belle Rose Ragins, John M. Cornwell
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 619
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0021-9010.92.4.1103

## Abstract

Stigma theory was used to examine the fears underlying the disclosure of a gay identity at work. Using a national sample of 534 gay, lesbian, and bisexual employees, this study examined the antecedents that affect the degree of disclosure of a gay identity at work and, for those who had not disclosed, the factors that influence their fears about full disclosure. Employees reported less fear and more disclosure when they worked in a group that was perceived as supportive and sharing their stigma.
