# Attachment in the preschool years: Theory, research, and intervention.

**Authors:** Mark T. Greenberg, E. Mark Cummings
**Year:** 1990
**Citations:** 2,172

## Abstract

This collection of original articles by leading specialists in child development brings together work from diverse backgrounds and disciplines to establish, for the first time, the importance of the preschool period (eighteen months to four years)for parent-child attachment relationships. Balancing theoretical, research-oriented, and clinical papers, Attachment in the Preschool Years provides valuable data and approaches for those working in a wide range of fields, including developmental psychology and psychopathology, child psychiatry, family therapy, pediatrics, nursing, and early childhood education.
