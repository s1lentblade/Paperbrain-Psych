# Emotional intelligence: New ability or eclectic traits?

**Authors:** John D. Mayer, David R. Caruso
**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 1,580
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066x.63.6.503

## Abstract

Some individuals have a greater capacity than others to carry out sophisticated information processing about emotions and emotion-relevant stimuli and to use this information as a guide to thinking and behavior. The authors have termed this set of abilities emotional intelligence (EI). Since the introduction of the concept, however, a schism has developed in which some researchers focus on EI as a distinct group of mental abilities, and other researchers instead study an eclectic mix of positive traits such as happiness, self-esteem, and optimism.
