## Abstract

For years it has been held that, when a given number of pain endings are injured and stimulated, a given degree of pain will be experienced; and that, for a given degree of pain, a given dose of morphine or similar agent is required. These propositions need to be examined. Any small boy in a fist fight feels no pain at the time of injury. This comes later, when his bloody nose begins to drip or his mother arrives to console him.
