# Neural Activity When People Solve Verbal Problems with Insight

**Authors:** Mark Beeman, John Kounios
**Year:** 2004
**Citations:** 1,029
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pbio.0020097

## Abstract

People sometimes solve problems with a unique process called insight, accompanied by an "Aha!" experience. It has long been unclear whether different cognitive and neural processes lead to insight versus noninsight solutions, or if solutions differ only in subsequent subjective feeling. Recent behavioral studies indicate distinct patterns of performance and suggest differential hemispheric involvement for insight and noninsight solutions. Subjects solved verbal problems, and after each correct solution indicated whether they solved with or without insight. We observed two objective neural correlates of insight.
