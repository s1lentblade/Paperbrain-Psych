**Citations:** 413

## Abstract

This chapter reports a study that selects a sample of smiles on the basis of the best substantiated marker of enjoyment (Duchenne's) and determines whether such smiles also showed the limited duration marker. The data are consistent with the proposal that smiles with the Duchenne marker act more like emotional facial actions. Another study addressed the question of whether the subtle markers of enjoyment can operate as social signals.
