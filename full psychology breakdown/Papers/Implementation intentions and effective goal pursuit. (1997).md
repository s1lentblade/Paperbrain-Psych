## Abstract

The theoretical distinction between goal intentions (&amp;quot;I intend to achieve-c&amp;quot;) and implementation intentions (&amp;quot;I intend to perform goal-directed behavior y when I encounter situation z &amp;quot; ; P. M. Gollwitzer, 1993) is explored by assessing the completion rate of various goal projects. In correla-tional Study 1, difficult goal intentions were completed about 3 times more often when participants had furnished them with implementation intentions. In experimental Study 2, all participants were assigned the same difficult goal intention, and half were instructed to form implementation intentions.
