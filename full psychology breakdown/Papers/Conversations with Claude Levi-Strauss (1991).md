## Abstract

At the age of eighty, one of the most influential yet reclusive intellectuals of the twentieth century consented his first interviews in nearly thirty years. Hailed by Le Figaro as event, the resulting conversations between Claude Levi-Strauss and Didier Eribon (a correspondent for Le Nouvel Observateur) reveal the great anthropologist speaking of his life and work with ease and humor.
