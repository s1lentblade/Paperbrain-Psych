# Body image across the life span in adult women: The role of self-objectification.

**Authors:** Marika Tiggemann, Jessica E. Lynch
**Year:** 2001
**Citations:** 747
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0012-1649.37.2.243

## Abstract

This study aimed to investigate women's body image across the entire life span from within the theoretical perspective provided by objectification theory (B. L. Fredrickson & T.-A. Roberts, 1997). In a cross-sectional study, a sample of 322 women ranging in age from 20 to 84 years completed a questionnaire measuring body dissatisfaction, self-objectification, and its proposed consequences. Although body dissatisfaction remained stable across the age range, self-objectification, habitual body monitoring, appearance anxiety, and disordered eating symptomatology all significantly decreased with age.
