# Job-related stress, social support, and burnout among classroom teachers.

**Year:** 1987
**Citations:** 534
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0021-9010.72.2.269

## Abstract

In this study we examined the effects of job-related stressful events and social support on burnout among teachers. We conducted a mail survey of a random sample of public school teachers in Iowa. Consistent with findings in previous research, teacher characteristics such as age, sex, and grade level taught were predictive of burnout. We also found that the number of stressful events experienced and social support were predictive of teacher burnout. Some evidence of the stress-moderating role of social support was also found.
