# Solving the Emotion Paradox: Categorization and the Experience of Emotion

**Authors:** Lisa Feldman Barrett
**Year:** 2006
**Citations:** 1,641
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1207/s15327957pspr1001_2

## Abstract

In this article, I introduce an emotion paradox: People believe that they know an emotion when they see it, and as a consequence assume that emotions are discrete events that can be recognized with some degree of accuracy, but scientists have yet to produce a set of clear and consistent criteria for indicating when an emotion is present and when it is not. I propose one solution to this paradox: People experience an emotion when they conceptualize an instance of affective feeling. In this view, the experience of emotion is an act of categorization, guided by embodied knowledge about emotion.
