## Abstract

Charlie Martin has shown that the simple conditional analysis of dispositional concepts (x is disposed at time t to give response r to stimulus s) is in error. This is due to finkish dispositions which are caused to disappear by the stimulus s. David Lewis has proposed an improved analysis which takes account of finkish dispositions by requiring that the appropriate causal bias remains for a sufficiently long time. In this paper I argue that Lewis's anlysis also fails. This is because of the possibility of antidotes.
