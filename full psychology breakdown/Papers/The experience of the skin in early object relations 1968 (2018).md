## Abstract

This chapter is concerned with the primal function of the skin of the baby and of its primal objects in relation to the most primitive binding together of parts of the personality not as yet differentiated from parts of the body. One year of observation of an immature young mother and her first baby showed a gradual improvement in the &quot;skin-container&quot; function up to twelve weeks. As the mother's tolerance to closeness to the baby increased, so did her need to excite the baby to manifestations of vitality lessen.
