# Datafied child welfare services: unpacking politics, economics and power

**Authors:** Joanna Redden, Harry Warne
**Year:** 2020
**Citations:** 82
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/01442872.2020.1724928

## Abstract

This article analyses three distinct child welfare data systems in England. We focus on child welfare as a contested area in public services where data systems are being used to inform decision-making and transforming governance. We advance the use of “data assemblage” as an analytical framework to detail how key political and economic factors influence the development of these data systems. We provide an empirically grounded demonstration of why child welfare data systems must not be considered neutral decision aid tools.
