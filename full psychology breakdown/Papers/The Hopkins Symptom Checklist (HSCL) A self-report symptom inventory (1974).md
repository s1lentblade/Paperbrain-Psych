# The Hopkins Symptom Checklist (HSCL): A self-report symptom inventory

**Authors:** Leonard R. Derogatis, Lino Covi
**Year:** 1974
**Citations:** 4,625

## Abstract

This report describes the historical evolution, development, rationale and validation of the Hopkins Symptom Checklist (HSCL), a self-report symptom inventory. The HSCL is comprised of 58 items which are representative of the symptom configurations commonly observed among outpatients. It is scored on five underlying symptom dimensions—sommatization, obsessive-compulsive, interpersonal sensitivity, anxiety and depression—which have been identified in repeated factor analyses. A series of studies have established the factorial invariance of the primary symptom dimensions, and substantial evidence is given in support of their construct validity.
