# Mobilising Discourses Handloom as Sustainable Socio-Technology

**Authors:** Annapurna Mamidipudi, Wiebe E. Bijker
**Year:** 2012
**Citations:** 89

## Abstract

Handloom is much more sustainable than common views and standard government policies recognise. Instead of a linear migration out of weaving into other forms of livelihood, weaving communities show a more strategic mobility - flexibly departing from and again returning to weaving, depending on circumstances. This mobility can be traced in weavers' discourses about their vulnerabilities and aspirations. This paper shows that the standard image of weaving as premodern, unproductive and unsustainable is produced by being trapped in a progress discourse, a poverty discourse, and a market discourse.
