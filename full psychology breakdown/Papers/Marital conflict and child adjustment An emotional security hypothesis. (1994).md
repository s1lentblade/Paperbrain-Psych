# Marital conflict and child adjustment: An emotional security hypothesis.

**Authors:** Patrick T. Davies, E. Mark Cummings
**Year:** 1994
**Citations:** 1,791
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.116.3.387

## Abstract

An emotional security hypothesis that builds on attachment theory is proposed to account for recent empirical findings on the impact of marital conflict on children and to provide directions for future research. Children's concerns about emotional security play a role in their regulation of emotional arousal and organization and in their motivation to respond in the face of marital conflict. Over time these response processes and internalized representations of parental relations that develop have implications for children's long-term adjustment.
