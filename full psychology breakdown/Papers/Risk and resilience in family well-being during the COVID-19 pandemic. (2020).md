# Risk and resilience in family well-being during the COVID-19 pandemic.

**Authors:** Heather Prime, Dillon T. Browne
**Year:** 2020
**Citations:** 2,162
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/amp0000660

## Abstract

The COVID-19 pandemic poses an acute threat to the well-being of children and families due to challenges related to social disruption such as financial insecurity, caregiving burden, and confinement-related stress (e.g., crowding, changes to structure, and routine). The consequences of these difficulties are likely to be longstanding, in part because of the ways in which contextual risk permeates the structures and processes of family systems. The current article draws from pertinent literature across topic areas of acute crises and long-term, cumulative risk to illustrate the multitude of ways in which the well-being of children and families may be at risk during COVID-19.
