# A lab test and algorithms for identifying clients at risk for treatment failure

**Authors:** Corinne R. Hannan, Scott W. Sutton
**Year:** 2004
**Citations:** 543
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/jclp.20108

## Abstract

Many branches of medicine rely heavily on lab tests to monitor client treatment response and use this information to modify their treatment. By contrast, those who offer psychological interventions seldom rely on formal assessments (lab tests) to monitor their clients' response to treatment. Data are presented that demonstrate that clinicians rarely accurately predict who will not benefit from psychotherapy. This finding is contrasted with the use of a questionnaire (lab test data) and decision rules on the basis of a client's expected progress.
