**Authors:** George Rasch

## Abstract

Abstract : Lately the statistical methods of psychometrics have been severely criticized in psychological quarters. Thus Skinner [1] maintains that if order is to be found in human and animal behavior, then it should be extracted from investigations into individuals, and that psychometric methods are inadequate for such purposes since they deal with groups of individuals. And as regards abnormal psychology Zubin [2] states: Recourse must be had to individual statistics, treating each patient as a separate universe.
