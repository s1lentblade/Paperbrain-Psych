# Learned Helplessness

**Authors:** Christopher Peterson, Martin E. P. Seligman
**Year:** 1993
**Citations:** 490
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/oso/9780195044669.001.0001

## Abstract

Abstract The psychological syndrome of learned helplessness is a uniquely modern phenomenon, deeply rooted in cultural concepts of personal power and security. This timely and valuable work examines learned helplessness with reference to the salient emphases in contemporary culture of individuality and personal control. An indispensable reference of interest to a broad spectrum of researchers in psychology.
