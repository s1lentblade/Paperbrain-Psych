# Conceptual Integration and Formal Expression

**Authors:** Mark Tunner, Gilles Fauconnier
**Year:** 1995
**Citations:** 602
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1207/s15327868ms1003_3

## Abstract

Abstract We pursue our exploration of conceptual blending and of the "many-space" model, which replaces the standard "two-domain" model. In blending, structure from two or more input mental spaces is projected to a separate "blended" space, which inherits partial structure from the inputs and has emergent structure of its own. New examples are presented. We show that meaning is not compositional in the usual sense and that blending operates to produce understandings of composite forms.
