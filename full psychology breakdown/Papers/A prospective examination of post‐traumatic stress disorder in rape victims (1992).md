# A prospective examination of post‐traumatic stress disorder in rape victims

**Authors:** Barbara O. Rothbaum, William A. Walsh
**Year:** 1992
**Citations:** 1,063
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/jts.2490050309

## Abstract

Abstract Post‐traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and related psychopathology were examined in 95 female rape victims beginning soon after the assault (mean = 12.64 days). Subjects were assessed weekly for 12 weeks. Ninety‐four percent of women met symptomatic criteria for PTSD at Assessment 1, decreasing to 65% at Assessment 4 (mean = 35 days postassault), and 47% at Assessment 12 (mean = 94 days postassault). PTSD and related psychopathology decreased sharply between Assessments 1 and 4 for all women.
