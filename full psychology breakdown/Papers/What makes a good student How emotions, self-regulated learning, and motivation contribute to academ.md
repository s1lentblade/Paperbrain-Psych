# What makes a good student? How emotions, self-regulated learning, and motivation contribute to academic achievement.

**Authors:** Carolina Mega, Rossana De Béni
**Year:** 2013
**Citations:** 970
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/a0033546

## Abstract

The authors propose a theoretical model linking emotions, self-regulated learning, and motivation to academic achievement. This model was tested with 5,805 undergraduate students. They completed the Self-Regulated Learning, Emotions, and Motivation Computerized Battery (LEM–B) composed of 3 self-report questionnaires: the Self-Regulated Learning Questionnaire (LQ), the Emotions Questionnaire (EQ), and the Motivation Questionnaire (MQ). The findings were consistent with the authors’ hypotheses and appeared to support all aspects of the proposed model.
