# A Comparison of Young, Middle-Aged, and Older Adult Treatment-Seeking Pathological Gamblers

**Authors:** Nancy M. Petry
**Year:** 2002
**Citations:** 400
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/geront/42.1.92

## Abstract

Purpose: Pathological gambling is an increasing public health concern, but very little is known about this disorder in older adults. This study evaluated gambling and psychosocial problems across age groups in treatment-seeking gamblers. Design and Methods: At intake to gambling treatment programs, 343 pathological gamblers completed the Addiction Severity Index (ASI) and gambling questionnaires. Participants were categorized by age into young adults (ages 18–35 years; n = 97), middle-aged adults (ages 36–55 years, n = 197), and older adults (aged older than 55 years, n = 49).
