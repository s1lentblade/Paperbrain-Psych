# Building maintenance strategy: a new management approach

**Authors:** R. Malcolm W. Horner, A.K. Munns
**Year:** 1997
**Citations:** 215
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1108/13552519710176881

## Abstract

Points out that increasing pressure to prolong the useful life of a building without compromising the objectives of maintenance has led to an increasing interest in methods of integrated maintenance management. As an alternative to budget‐driven maintenance strategies, develops a new approach to selecting an appropriate maintenance strategy which relies on determining the consequences of failure of every item in the building, and determining a suitable strategy for each one. Analyses the relative advantages and disadvantages of corrective, preventive and condition‐based strategies. Advocates a novel, systematic approach to the management of building maintenance.
