## Abstract

This study was an examination of the relationship of lexical richness to the quality of English as a second language (ESL) learners’ oral narratives. A computational system was designed to automate the measurement of 3 dimensions of lexical richness, that is, lexical density, sophistication, and variation, using 25 different metrics proposed in the language acquisition literature.
