**Authors:** Margit C. Ockenfels, Arthur A. Stone
**Year:** 1995
**Citations:** 297
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/00006842-199509000-00008

## Abstract

This study examined the effect of chronic stress associated with unemployment on the magnitude of salivary cortisol excretion, on the diurnal rhythm of cortisol, and on cortisol reactivity to acute naturalistic stressors using Experience Sampling Methodology (ESM). Employed (N = 60) and unemployed (N = 60) subjects were studied for 2 days. Subjects were beeped 6 times per day by a preprogrammed wristwatch to assess acute stressors; 25 minutes after each ESM-beep, subjects were beeped a second time for saliva samples.
