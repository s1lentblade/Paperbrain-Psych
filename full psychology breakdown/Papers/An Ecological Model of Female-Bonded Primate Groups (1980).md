# An Ecological Model of Female-Bonded Primate Groups

**Authors:** Richard W. Wrangham
**Year:** 1980
**Citations:** 1,967
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1163/156853980x00447

## Abstract

1. Multi-female groups of primates fall into two main classes, (a) female-bonded (FB) and (b) non-female-bonded (non-FB). A model is presented to account for the evolution of FB groups in terms of ecological pressures on female relationships. 2. The model suggests that FB groups have evolved as a result of competition for high-quality food patches containing a limited number of feeding sites. Groups are viewed as being based on cooperative relationships among females. These relationships are beneficial because cooperators act together to supplant others from preferred food patches. 3.
