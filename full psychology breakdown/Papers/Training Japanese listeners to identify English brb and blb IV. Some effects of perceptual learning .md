# Training Japanese listeners to identify English /<b>r</b>/ and /<b>l</b>/: IV. Some effects of perceptual learning on speech production

**Authors:** Ann R. Bradlow, Yoh’ichi Tohkura
**Year:** 1997
**Citations:** 829
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1121/1.418276

## Abstract

This study investigated the effects of training in/r/-/l/ perceptual identification on /r/-/l/ production by adult Japanese speakers. Subjects were recorded producing English words that contrast /r/ and /l/ before and after participating in an extended period of /r/-/l/ identification training using a high-variability presentation format. All subjects showed significant perceptual learning as a result of the training program, and this perceptual learning generalized to novel items spoken by new talkers.
