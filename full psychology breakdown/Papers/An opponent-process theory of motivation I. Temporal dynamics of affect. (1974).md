# An opponent-process theory of motivation: I. Temporal dynamics of affect.

**Authors:** Richard L. Solomon, John Corbit
**Year:** 1974
**Citations:** 1,688
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/h0036128

## Abstract

A new theory of motivation is described along with its applications to addiction and aversion. The theory assumes that many hedonic, affective, or emotional states are automatically opposed by central nervous system mechanisms which reduce the intensity of hedonic feelings, both pleasant and aversive. The opponent processes for most hedonic states are strength-ened by use and are weakened by disuse. These simple assumptions lead to deductions of many known facts about acquired motivation. In addition, the theory suggests several new lines of research on motivation.
