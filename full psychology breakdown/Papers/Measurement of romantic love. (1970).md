## Abstract

This study reports Ihc initial results of an attempt to introduce and validate a social-psychological construct oi &amp;quot; romantic love. Starting with the assumption that love is an interpersonal attitude, an internally consistent papcr-and-pencil love scale was developed. The conception of romantic love included three com-ponents: affiliative and dependent need, a predisposition to help, and an orienta-tion of exclusiveness and absorption. Love-scale scores were only moderately correlated with scores on a parallel scale of &amp;quot;liking, &amp;quot; which reflected a more traditional conception of interpersonal attraction. The validity of the love scale was assessed in a questionnaire
