# Educational video game for juvenile diabetes: results of a controlled trial

**Authors:** Stephanie Brown, David J. Pasta
**Year:** 1997
**Citations:** 482

## Abstract

Packy & Marlon, an interactive video game designed to improve self-care among children and adolescents with diabetes, was evaluated in a six-month randomized controlled trial. In the game, players take the role of animated characters who manage their diabetes by monitoring blood glucose, taking insulin injections, and choosing foods, while setting out to save a diabetes summer camp from marauding rats and mice who have stolen the diabetes supplies. Study participants were patients aged 8 to 16 from two separate diabetes clinics.
