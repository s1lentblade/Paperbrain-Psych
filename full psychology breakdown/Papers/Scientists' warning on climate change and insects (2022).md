# Scientists' warning on climate change and insects

**Authors:** Jeffrey A. Harvey, Steven L. Chown
**Year:** 2022
**Citations:** 523
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/ecm.1553

## Abstract

Abstract Climate warming is considered to be among the most serious of anthropogenic stresses to the environment, because it not only has direct effects on biodiversity, but it also exacerbates the harmful effects of other human‐mediated threats. The associated consequences are potentially severe, particularly in terms of threats to species preservation, as well as in the preservation of an array of ecosystem services provided by biodiversity. Among the most affected groups of animals are insects—central components of many ecosystems—for which climate change has pervasive effects from individuals to communities.
