## Abstract

At the turn of the century, new dance practices provided vehicles for social engagement and offered grounds for interchange and jockeying between class groups. Along these lines of engagement, dance also provided a medium for negotiating matters of gender. Women seized the moment to develop new dance practices. On the East Coast and in the Midwest, middle class women found platforms for dance innovation in the settlement house movement. They taught classes in folk, gymnastic, and historical dancing styles, attempting to offset the physical and cultural effects of mass production, industry and urban crowding.
