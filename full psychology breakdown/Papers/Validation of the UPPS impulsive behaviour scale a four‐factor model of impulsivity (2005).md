# Validation of the UPPS impulsive behaviour scale: a four‐factor model of impulsivity

**Authors:** Stephen P. Whiteside, Sarah K. Reynolds
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 1,010
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/per.556

## Abstract

The current study attempts to clarify the multi‐faceted nature of impulsivity through the use of the four‐factor UPPS Impulsive Behaviour scale. In order to build the nomological network surrounding this scale, the UPPS was administered to individuals with borderline personality disorder (BPD), pathological gamblers (PG), alcohol abusers (divided into two groups based on the presence of antisocial features), and a control group. Several of the UPPS scales (e.g. Urgency, lack of Premeditation, and Sensation Seeking) differentiated the BPD, PG, and alcohol abusers with antisocial features from a group of non‐antisocial alcohol abusers and a control group.
