## Abstract

Abstract This paper contributes to a reappraisal of the sociological significance of the medical record. Building upon re‐evaluations of the notion of ‘representation’ in ethnomethodological and science and technology studies, it attempts to portray the medical record as an artefact which mediates the social relations that act and work through it. Through practices of reading and writing, it is argued, the medical record figures as a fundamental, constitutive element of medical practice.
