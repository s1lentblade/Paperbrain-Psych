# Not all emotions are created equal: The negativity bias in social-emotional development.

**Authors:** Amrisha Vaish, Amanda L. Woodward
**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 1,151
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.134.3.383

## Abstract

There is ample empirical evidence for an asymmetry in the way that adults use positive versus negative information to make sense of their world; specifically, across an array of psychological situations and tasks, adults display a negativity bias, or the propensity to attend to, learn from, and use negative information far more than positive information. This bias is argued to serve critical evolutionarily adaptive functions, but its developmental presence and ontogenetic emergence have never been seriously considered.
