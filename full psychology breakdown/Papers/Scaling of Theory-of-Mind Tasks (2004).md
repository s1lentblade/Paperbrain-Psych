# Scaling of Theory-of-Mind Tasks

**Authors:** Henry M. Wellman, David Liu
**Year:** 2004
**Citations:** 1,926
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-8624.2004.00691.x

## Abstract

Two studies address the sequence of understandings evident in preschoolers' developing theory of mind. The first, preliminary study provides a meta-analysis of research comparing different types of mental state understandings (e.g., desires vs. beliefs, ignorance vs. false belief). The second, primary study tests a theory-of-mind scale for preschoolers. In this study 75 children (aged 2 years, 11 months to 6 years, 6 months) were tested on 7 tasks tapping different aspects of understanding persons' mental states. Responses formed a consistent developmental progression, where for most children if they passed a later item they passed all earlier items as well, as confirmed by Guttman and Rasch
