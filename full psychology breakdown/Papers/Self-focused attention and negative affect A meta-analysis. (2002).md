# Self-focused attention and negative affect: A meta-analysis.

**Authors:** Nilly Mor, Jennifer R. Winquist
**Year:** 2002
**Citations:** 1,041
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.128.4.638

## Abstract

This meta-analysis synthesized 226 effect sizes reflecting the relation between self-focused attention and negative affect (depression, anxiety, negative mood). The results demonstrate the multifaceted nature of self-focused attention and elucidate major controversies in the field. Overall, self-focus was associated with negative affect. Several moderators qualified this relationship. Self-focus and negative affect were more strongly related in clinical and female-dominated samples. Rumination yielded stronger effect sizes than nonruminative self-focus. Self-focus on positive self-aspects and following a positive event were related to lower negative affect.
