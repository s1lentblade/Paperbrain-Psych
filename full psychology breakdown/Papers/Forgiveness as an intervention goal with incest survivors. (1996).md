# Forgiveness as an intervention goal with incest survivors.

**Authors:** Suzanne Freedman, Robert D. Enright
**Year:** 1996
**Citations:** 527
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037//0022-006x.64.5.983

## Abstract

An intervention, with forgiveness toward their abuser as the goal, was implemented with 12 female incest survivors. The women, from a midwestern city, were 24 to 54 years old, and all were Caucasian. A yoked, randomized experimental and control group design was used. The participants were randomly assigned to an experimental group (receiving the forgiveness intervention immediately) or a waiting-list control group (receiving the intervention when their matched experimental counterpart finished the intervention).
