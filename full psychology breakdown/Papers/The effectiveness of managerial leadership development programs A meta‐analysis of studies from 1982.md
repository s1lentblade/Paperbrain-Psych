# The effectiveness of managerial leadership development programs: A meta‐analysis of studies from 1982 to 2001

**Authors:** Doris B. Collins, Elwood F. Holton
**Year:** 2004
**Citations:** 511
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/hrdq.1099

## Abstract

Abstract Eighty‐three studies from 1982 to 2001 with formal training interventions were integrated via meta‐analytic techniques to determine the effectiveness of interventions in their enhancement of performance, knowledge, and expertise at the individual, team or group, or organizational level. The studies were separated by research design, with the outcome measure of the intervention as the unit of analysis. The effect size for knowledge outcomes ranged from .96 to 1.37; expertise outcomes from .35 to 1.01; and system outcomes averaged .39.
