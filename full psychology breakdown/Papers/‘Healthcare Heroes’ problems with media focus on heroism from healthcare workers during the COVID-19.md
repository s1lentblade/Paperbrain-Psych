**Authors:** Caitríona Cox
**Year:** 2020
**Citations:** 289
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1136/medethics-2020-106398

## Abstract

During the COVID-19 pandemic, the media have repeatedly praised healthcare workers for their 'heroic' work. Although this gratitude is undoubtedly appreciated by many, we must be cautious about overuse of the term 'hero' in such discussions. The challenges currently faced by healthcare workers are substantially greater than those encountered in their normal work, and it is understandable that the language of heroism has been evoked to praise them for their actions. Yet such language can have potentially negative consequences.
