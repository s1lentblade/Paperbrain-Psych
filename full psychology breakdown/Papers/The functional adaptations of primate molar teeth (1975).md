# The functional adaptations of primate molar teeth

**Authors:** Richard F. Kay
**Year:** 1975
**Citations:** 730
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/ajpa.1330430207

## Abstract

Measurements were taken on the upper and lower molars of 37 species of primates and one tupaiid to assess the relative importance of shearing, crushing and grinding features. Significant correlations were found between pairs of allometrically standardized dimensions which measure the same molar function (shearing, crushing, or grinding). Correlations between pairs of dimensions which do not measure the same function are not significant. Second molar adaptations for shearing, crushing, and grinding, as well as the length of the second lower molar, and the total surface of the post-canine dentition are negatively allometric with respect to metabolic rate.
