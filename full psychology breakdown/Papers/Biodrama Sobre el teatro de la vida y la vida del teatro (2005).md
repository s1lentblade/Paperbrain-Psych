## Abstract

The evolution of modern art can be understood as a search for a dialogue increasingly associated with a non-intellectualized reality, at the same time that an excess of fictions suggests certain insufficiencies. The cycle of biodramas sponsored by Viviana Tellas through the public theatre of Buenos Aires represented an invitation to different theatre practitioners to work on living people in Argentina. The interesting thing is that, to talk about life in the raw sense, these works were obligated to reflect on their own theatrical functions, thus manifesting the theatricality implicit in life itself.
