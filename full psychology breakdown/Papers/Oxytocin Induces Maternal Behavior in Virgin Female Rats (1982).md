# Oxytocin Induces Maternal Behavior in Virgin Female Rats

**Authors:** Cort A. Pedersen, Arthur J. Prange
**Year:** 1982
**Citations:** 772
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1126/science.7071605

## Abstract

Intracerebroventricular administration of oxytocin to virgin female rats that had been ovariectomized and primed with estrogen 48 hours previously induced a rapid onset of full maternal behavior. The maternal behavior persisted and its incidence was dose-related. Tocinoic acid, the ring structure of oxytocin, also rapidly induced the onset of persistent, full maternal behavior. Arginine vasopressin induced persistent maternal behavior, but this behavior had a later onset. Prostaglandin F2 alpha induced strong partial maternal behavior, which showed early onset but did not persist.
