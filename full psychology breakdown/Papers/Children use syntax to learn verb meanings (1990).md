# Children use syntax to learn verb meanings

**Authors:** Letitia Naigles
**Year:** 1990
**Citations:** 875
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1017/s0305000900013817

## Abstract

Verb learning is clearly a function of observation of real-world contingencies; however, it is argued that such observational information is insufficient to account fully for vocabulary acquisition. This paper provides an experimental validation of Landau & Gleitman's (1985) syntactic bootstrapping procedure; namely, that children may use syntactic information to learn new verbs. Pairs of actions were presented simultaneously with a nonsense verb in one of two syntactic structures. The actions were subsequently separated, and the children (MA = 2;1) were asked to select which action was the referent for the verb.
