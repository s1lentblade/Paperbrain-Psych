# Over-scripting CSCL: The risks of blending collaborative learning with instructional design

**Authors:** Pierre Dillenbourg
**Year:** 2002
**Citations:** 891

## Abstract

Abstract. Free collaboration does not systematically produce learning. One way to enhance the effectiveness of collaborative learning is to structure interactions by engaging students in well-defined scripts. A collaboration script is a set of instructions prescribing how students should form groups, how they should interact and collaborate and how they should solve the problem. In computer-supported collaborative learning (CSCL), the script is reified in the interface of the learning environment. This contribution dismantles the concept of script.
