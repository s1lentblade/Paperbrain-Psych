**Authors:** Masashi Soga, Keisuke Hanaki
**Year:** 2016
**Citations:** 264
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph13060529

## Abstract

Children are becoming less likely to have direct contact with nature. This ongoing loss of human interactions with nature, the extinction of experience, is viewed as one of the most fundamental obstacles to addressing global environmental challenges. However, the consequences for biodiversity conservation have been examined very little.
