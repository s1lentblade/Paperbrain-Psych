# Discourse, Consciousness, and Time: The Flow and Displacement of Conscious Experience in Speaking and Writing

**Authors:** Wallace Chafe
**Year:** 1994
**Citations:** 2,114

## Abstract

Wallace Chafe demonstrates how the study of language and consciousness together can provide an unexpectedly broad understanding of the way the mind works. Relying on analyses of conversational speech, written fiction and nonfiction, the North American Indian language Seneca, and the music of Mozart and of the Seneca people, he investigates both the flow of ideas through consciousness and the displacement of consciousness by way of memory and imagination.
