# Gender differences in adult foot shape: implications for shoe design

**Authors:** Roshna E. Wunderlich, Peter R. Cavanagh
**Year:** 2001
**Citations:** 287
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/00005768-200104000-00015

## Abstract

This study demonstrates that female feet and legs are not simply scaled-down versions of male feet but rather differ in a number of shape characteristics, particularly at the arch, the lateral side of the foot, the first toe, and the ball of the foot. These differences should be taken into account in the design and manufacture of women's sport shoes.
