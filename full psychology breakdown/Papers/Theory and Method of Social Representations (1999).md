# Theory and Method of Social Representations

**Authors:** Wolfgang Wagner, Diana Rose
**Year:** 1999
**Citations:** 532

## Abstract

This paper gives an overview of social representation theory, definitions of the key terms and of the social processes leading to a representation and to social identity. Six empirical studies are presented and details of their methods and findings are given to illustrate this social psychological approach. These studies are about the ontogenesis of gender, the public sphere in Brazil, madness on British television, images of androgyny in Switzerland, individualism and democracy in post‐communist Europe and metaphorical thinking about conception.
