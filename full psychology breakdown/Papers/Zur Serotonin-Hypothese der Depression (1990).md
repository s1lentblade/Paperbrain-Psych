## Abstract

Current theoretical and experimental developments in serotonin research extend from the differentiated description of central cytoarchitectonic structures over the identification and characterization of multiple receptor subtypes by pharmacological and molecular biological methods to the elucidation of neurobiochemical and physiological mechanisms of interneuronal communication and postreceptor signal transduction. These advances parallel the modification and optimization of various strategies for researching the relevance of central serotonergic neurotransmission in the aetiopathogenesis of affective disorders.
