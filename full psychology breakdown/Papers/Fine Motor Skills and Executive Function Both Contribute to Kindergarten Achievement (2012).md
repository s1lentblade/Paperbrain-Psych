# Fine Motor Skills and Executive Function Both Contribute to Kindergarten Achievement

**Authors:** Claire E. Cameron, Frederick J. Morrison
**Year:** 2012
**Citations:** 493
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-8624.2012.01768.x

## Abstract

This study examined the contribution of executive function (EF) and multiple aspects of fine motor skills to achievement on 6 standardized assessments in a sample of middle-socioeconomic status kindergarteners. Three- and 4-year-olds' (n=213) fine and gross motor skills were assessed in a home visit before kindergarten, EF was measured at fall of kindergarten, and Woodcock-Johnson III Tests of Academic Achievement were administered at fall and spring. Correlations indicated that EF and fine motor skills appeared distinct.
