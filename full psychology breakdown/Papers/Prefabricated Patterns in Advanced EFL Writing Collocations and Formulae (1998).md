## Abstract

Abstract Since the mid-I98os, the use of prefabricated language (‘prefabs’) has become a major focus of interest in EFL, arguably for three main reasons. First, the emergence of the concept of lexico-grammar, inspired by Halliday and Sinclair, has promoted the syntagmatic investigation of lexis. The traditional association between syntagmatics and grammar, on the one hand, and paradigmatics and lexis, on the other, which was challenged by Michael Halliday as early as 1966, is now clearly a thing of the past.
