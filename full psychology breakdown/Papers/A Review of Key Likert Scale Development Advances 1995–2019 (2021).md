## Abstract

Developing self-report Likert scales is an essential part of modern psychology. However, it is hard for psychologists to remain apprised of best practices as methodological developments accumulate. To address this, this current paper offers a selective review of advances in Likert scale development that have occurred over the past 25 years. We reviewed six major measurement journals (e.g., <i>Psychological Methods</i>, <i>Educational</i>, <i>and Psychological Measurement</i>) between the years 1995-2019 and identified key advances, ultimately including 40 papers and offering written summaries of each.
