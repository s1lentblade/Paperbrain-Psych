# The effectiveness of psychotherapy: The Consumer Reports study.

**Authors:** Martin E. P. Seligman
**Year:** 1995
**Citations:** 1,260
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037//0003-066x.50.12.965

## Abstract

Consumer Reports (1995, November) published an article which concluded that patients benefited very substantially from psychotherapy, that long-term treatment did considerably better than short-term treatment, and that psychotherapy alone did not differ in effectiveness from medication plus psychotherapy. Furthermore, no specific modality of psychotherapy did better than any other for any disorder; psychologists, psychiatrists, and social workers did not differ in their effectiveness as treaters; and all did better than marriage counselors and long-term family doctoring.
