# Roles, Risks, and Benefits of Peer Mentoring Relationships in Higher Education

**Authors:** Janet Colvin, Marinda Ashman
**Year:** 2010
**Citations:** 433
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/13611261003678879

## Abstract

Successful peer mentoring in university settings is the result of relationships among students, mentors, and instructors. Findings from this study indicate that even in programs where training is ongoing and established, assumptions cannot be made about the understanding of the roles, risks, and benefits involved in such relationships. This study demonstrates that students, instructors, and mentors all have different perspectives about a mentor’s role and how that role should be enacted. Connecting link, peer leader, learning coach, student advocate, and trusted friend were identified as predominant roles enacted by mentors.
