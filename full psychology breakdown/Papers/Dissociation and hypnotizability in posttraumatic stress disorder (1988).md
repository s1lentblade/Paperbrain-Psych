# Dissociation and hypnotizability in posttraumatic stress disorder

**Authors:** David Spiegel, H E Dondershine
**Year:** 1988
**Citations:** 382
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1176/ajp.145.3.301

## Abstract

The authors compared the hypnotizability of 65 Vietnam veteran patients with posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD) to that of a normal control group and four patient samples using the Hypnotic Induction Profile. The patients with PTSD had significantly higher hypnotizability scores than patients with diagnoses of schizophrenia (N = 23); major depression, bipolar disorder--depressed, and dysthymic disorder (N = 56); and generalized anxiety disorder (N = 18) and the control sample (N = 83). This finding supports the hypothesis that dissociative phenomena are mobilized as defenses both during and after traumatic experiences.
