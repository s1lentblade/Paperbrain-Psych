# Long-Term Consequences of Child Abuse and Neglect on Adult Economic Well-Being

**Authors:** Janet Currie, Cathy Spatz Widom
**Year:** 2010
**Citations:** 734
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/1077559509355316

## Abstract

Child abuse and neglect represent major threats to child health and well-being; however, little is known about consequences for adult economic outcomes. Using a prospective cohort design, court substantiated cases of childhood physical and sexual abuse and neglect during 1967—1971 were matched with nonabused and nonneglected children and followed into adulthood (mean age 41). Outcome measures of economic status and productivity were assessed in 2003—2004 (N = 807). Results indicate that adults with documented histories of childhood abuse and/or neglect have lower levels of education, employment, earnings, and fewer assets as adults, compared to matched control children.
