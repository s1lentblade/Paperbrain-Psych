# Positive affect and the other side of coping.

**Authors:** Susan Folkman, Judith T. Moskowitz
**Year:** 2000
**Citations:** 542
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037//0003-066x.55.6.647

## Abstract

Although research on coping over the past 30 years has produced convergent evidence about the functions of coping and the factors that influence it, psychologists still have a great deal to learn about how coping mechanisms affect diverse outcomes. One of the reasons more progress has not been made is the almost exclusive focus on negative outcomes in the stress process. Coping theory and research need to consider positive outcomes as well.
