**Year:** 2007

## Abstract

The practice of psychology can be demanding, challenging, and emotionally taxing. Failure to adequately attend to one’s own psychological wellness and self-care can place the psychologist at risk for impaired professional functioning. An ongoing focus on self-care is essential for the prevention of burnout and for maintaining one’s own psychological wellness. Salient aspects of self-care are discussed, including the ethical imperative of addressing self-care throughout one’s career.
