# A Comparison of Approaches to Forming Composite Measures in Structural Equation Models

**Authors:** Ronald S. Landis, Paul E. Tesluk
**Year:** 2000
**Citations:** 1,127

## Abstract

A common practice in applications of structural equation modeling techniques is to create composite measures from individual items. The purpose of this article was to provide an empirical comparison of several composite formation methods on model fit. Data from 1, 177 public school teachers were used to test a model of union commitment in which alternative composite formation methods were used to specify the measurement components of the model. Bootstrapping procedures were used to generate data for two additional sample sizes.
