# An Experiment on Public Speaking Anxiety in Response to Three Different Types of Virtual Audience

**Authors:** David-Paul Pertaub, Chris Barker
**Year:** 2002
**Citations:** 486
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1162/105474602317343668

## Abstract

This paper describes an experiment to assess the anxiety responses of people giving 5 min. presentations to virtual audiences consisting of eight male avatars. There were three different types of audience behavior: an emotionally neutral audience that remained static throughout the talk, a positive audience that exhibited friendly and appreciative behavior towards the speaker, and a negative audience that exhibited hostile and bored expressions throughout the talk. A second factor was immersion: half of the forty subjects experienced the virtual seminar room through a head-tracked, head-mounted display and the remainder on a desktop system.
