## Abstract

A new model of group development suggests that groups &amp;apos; attention to time and pacing is an important catalyst of their progress through cre-ative projects. In this laboratory study, groups were videotaped as they produced creative products and then interviewed about replays of se-lected portions of the tapes. Participants &amp;apos; efforts to pace themselves were explored in depth, with special focus on a key feature of the model, a major transition in groups &amp;apos; approach toward their work at the midpoint of their allotted time.
