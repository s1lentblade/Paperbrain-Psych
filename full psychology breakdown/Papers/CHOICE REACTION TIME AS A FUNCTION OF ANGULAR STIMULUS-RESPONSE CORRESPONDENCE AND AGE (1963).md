# CHOICE REACTION TIME AS A FUNCTION OF ANGULAR STIMULUS-RESPONSE CORRESPONDENCE AND AGE

**Authors:** Jessica Simon, J D Wolf
**Year:** 1963
**Citations:** 298
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/00140136308930679

## Abstract

Abstract This study was concerned with the effect of varying the angular orientation of a display on the choice reaction times of two age groups ; a younger group ranging in age from 20 to 30 and an older group aged from 65 to 86. Two stimulus lights mounted on a vertical circular panel were rotated counterclockwise so that the lights formed angles of 0°, 45°, 90°, 135°, and 180° with the horizontal. Changing the spatial relationship of the stimulus lights relative to the fixed position of two response keys increased the complexity of the task and provided a means for testing the hypothesis that age differences in reaction time increase with increasing task difficulty.
