# Effects of 'Seductive Details' on Macroprocessing and Microprocessing in Adults and Children

**Authors:** Ruth Garner, Cynthia White
**Year:** 1989
**Citations:** 400
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1207/s1532690xci0601_2

## Abstract

Twenty adults were asked to read a three-paragraph expository text on differences among insects. Information in the text had been rated for importance and interestingness. Half of the adults read the text with "seductive details" (propositions presenting interesting, but unimportant, information), half without. After reading, the adults recalled the important information (a macroprocessing task), rated the text for overall interestingness, reported the single most interesting piece of information read, and matched pictures of animals on the basis of differences mentioned in text (a microprocessing task).
