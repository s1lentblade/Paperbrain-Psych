## Abstract

The fundamental texts of the great classical period modern logic, some of them never before available English translation, are here gathered together for the first time. Modern logic, heralded by Leibniz, may be said to have been initiated by Boole, De Morgan, and Jevons, but it was the publication 1879 of Gottlob Frege's Begriffsschrift that opened a great epoch the history of logic by presenting, full-fledged form, the propositional calculus and quantification theory. Frege's book, translated its entirety, begins the present volume.
