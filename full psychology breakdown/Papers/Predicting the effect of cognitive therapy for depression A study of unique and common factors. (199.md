# Predicting the effect of cognitive therapy for depression: A study of unique and common factors.

**Authors:** Louis G. Castonguay, Adele M. Hayes
**Year:** 1996
**Citations:** 717
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-006x.64.3.497

## Abstract

The ability of several process variables to predict therapy outcome was tested with 30 depressed clients who received cognitive therapy with or without medication. Two types of process variables were studied: 1 variable that is unique to cognitive therapy and 2 variables that this approach is assumed to share with other forms of treatment. The client's improvement was found to be predicted by the 2 common factors measured: the therapeutic alliance and the client's emotional involvement (experiencing).
