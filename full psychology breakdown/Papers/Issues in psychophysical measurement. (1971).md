## Abstract

Two classes of ratio-scaling procedures are outlined—magnitude matching and ratio matching—and their assets and liabilities are noted. Partition-scaling procedures, which are supposedly designed for interval scaling, pro-duce results that can be described by a power function with a virtual or &amp;quot;as if &amp;quot; exponent. Since the virtual exponent is smaller than the actual exponent of the continuum, the category scale is nonlinear. The virtual exponent provides a convenient descriptor of several kinds of partition operations.
