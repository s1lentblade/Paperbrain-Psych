## Abstract

Gordon Allport (1954) has described the attitude concept as &amp;quot;the primary building stone in the edifice of social psychology [p. 45], &amp;quot; and the extensive attitude literature in the past 20 years supports this contention. Stimulated primarily by the cognitive consistency theories, thousands of pages have been written recent-ly on attitude formation and change. One possible reason for the popularity of the attitude concept is that social psychologists have assumed that attitudes have something to do with social behavior.
