# The Out-of-the-Loop Performance Problem and Level of Control in Automation

**Authors:** Mica R. Endsley, Esin Kırış
**Year:** 1995
**Citations:** 1,297
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1518/001872095779064555

## Abstract

The out-of-the-loop performance problem, a major potential consequence of automation, leaves operators of automated systems handicapped in their ability to take over manual operations in the event of automation failure. This is attributed to a possible loss of skills and of situation awareness (SA) arising from vigilance and complacency problems, a shift from active to passive information processing, and change in feedback provided to the operator. We studied the automation of a navigation task using an expert system and demonstrated that low SA corresponded with out-of-the-loop performance decrements in decision time following a failure of the expert system.
