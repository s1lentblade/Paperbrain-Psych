## Abstract

Abstract This book explores the psychological, social, and economic facts of life for a group of discharged mental hospital patients living in Madison, Wisconsin. It both explicitly and inferentially raises questions about the rationale for deinstitutionalization. An anthropological field study, the work is the product of two years of intensive observation. It is a revealing enquiry into the lives of people whose welfare the United States has sought to promote by revolutionizing its system of psychiatric care.
