# Patterns of participation in recreational and leisure activities among children with complex physical disabilities

**Authors:** Mary Law, Steven Hanna
**Year:** 2006
**Citations:** 496

## Abstract

Children with physical disabilities are at increased risk of limitations to participation in everyday activities. This study describes research examining the participation of children in day-to-day formal and informal activities (excluding mandated academic schooling). Using the Children's Assessment of Participation and Enjoyment (CAPE) measure, data on participation patterns were collected from 427 children (229 males, 198 females; mean age 10 y [SD 2 y 4 mo]; range 6-14 y) with physical limitations and from their families.
