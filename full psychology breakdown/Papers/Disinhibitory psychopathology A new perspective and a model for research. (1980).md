## Abstract

The syndrome produced by lesion of the septum in animals can serve as a functional research model of human disinhibitory psychopathology. Disin-hibitory psychopathology appears to span several traditionally separate psy-chological categories—psychopathy, hysteria, hyperactivity, antisocial and im-pulsive personality, and alcoholism. It is proposed that these categories are separate manifestations of the same genetic diathesis and that the &amp;quot;septal syn-drome &amp;quot; may constitute a valid model of behavioral aspects of this diathesis.
