## Abstract

Long uninterrupted sedentary periods, independent of total sedentary time, are risk factors for poor health. There is little objective data relating to workplace sedentary behaviour and adherence to current recommendations. The sitting behaviour of office workers (n = 83) was quantified objectively using body-worn accelerometers (activPAL™) over a working week. Adherence to three different recommendations (maximum length of a sitting event of: 20 min; 30 min; 55 min) were assessed.
