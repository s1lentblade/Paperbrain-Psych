## Abstract

An essential part of the psychical disposition to neurosis lies in the delay in educating the sexual instincts to pay regard to reality and, as a corollary, in the conditions which make this delay possible. With the introduction of the reality principle one species of thought-activity was split off; it was kept free from reality-testing and remained subordinated to the pleasure principle alone. Education can be described without more ado as an incitement to the conquest of the pleasure principle, and to is replacement by the reality principle; it seeks, that is, to lend its help to the developmental process which affects the ego.
