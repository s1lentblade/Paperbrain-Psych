# A social-cognitive approach to motivation and personality.

**Authors:** Carol S. Dweck, Ellen L. Leggett
**Year:** 1988
**Citations:** 9,091
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295x.95.2.256

## Abstract

Past work has documented and described major patterns of adaptive and maladaptive behavior: the mastery-oriented and the helpless patterns. In this article, we present a research-based model that accounts for these patterns in terms of underlying psychological processes. The model specifies how individuals &amp;apos; implicit theories orient them toward particular goals and how these goals set up the different patterns. Indeed, we show how each feature (cognitive, affective, and behavioral) of the adaptive and maladaptive patterns can be seen to follow directly from different goals. We then exam-ine the generality of the model and use it to illuminate phenomena in a wide variety of domains.
