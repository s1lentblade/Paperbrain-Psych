# A rapid review of the impact of COVID-19 on the mental health of healthcare workers: implications for supporting psychological well-being

**Authors:** Johannes H. De Kock, Chris O’Malley
**Year:** 2021
**Citations:** 934
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s12889-020-10070-3

## Abstract

The evidence to date suggests that female nurses with close contact with COVID-19 patients may have the most to gain from efforts aimed at supporting psychological well-being. However, inconsistencies in findings and a lack of data collected outside of hospital settings, suggest that we should not exclude any groups when addressing psychological well-being in health and social care workers. Whilst psychological interventions aimed at enhancing resilience in the individual may be of benefit, it is evident that to build a resilient workforce, occupational and environmental factors must be addressed.
