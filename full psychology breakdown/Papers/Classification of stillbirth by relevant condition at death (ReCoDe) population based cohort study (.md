# Classification of stillbirth by relevant condition at death (ReCoDe): population based cohort study

**Authors:** Jason Gardosi, Ann Tonks
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 552
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1136/bmj.38629.587639.7c

## Abstract

The new ReCoDe classification system reduces the predominance of stillbirths currently categorised as unexplained. Fetal growth restriction is a common antecedent of stillbirth, but its high prevalence is hidden by current classification systems. This finding has profound implications for maternity services, and raises the question whether some hitherto "unexplained" stillbirths may be avoidable.
