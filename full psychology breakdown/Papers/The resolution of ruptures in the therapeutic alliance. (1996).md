## Abstract

This article outlines some of the main features of a research program on ruptures in the therapeutic alliance and reports some of the major findings to date. A rupture in the therapeutic alliance is a deterioration in the quality of the relationship between patient and therapist; it is an interpersonal marker that indicates a critical opportunity for exploring and understanding the processes that maintain a maladaptive interpersonal schema. Following the task-analytic research paradigin, a preliminary model of the resolution process was developed and then tested and revised with 2 different data sets.
