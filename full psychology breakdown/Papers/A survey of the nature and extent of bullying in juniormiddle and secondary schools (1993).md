# A survey of the nature and extent of bullying in junior/middle and secondary schools

**Authors:** Irene Whitney, Peter K. Smith
**Year:** 1993
**Citations:** 1,288
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/0013188930350101

## Abstract

Summary As part of a survey service developed to assess bullying in schools, anonymous questionnaires were given to over 6,000 pupils in 17 junior/middle and seven secondary schools in the Sheffield LEA. The results are analysed in terms of frequencies of being bullied, and bullying others; year differences; gender differences; types of bullying; where bullying occurs; whether teachers and parents are informed; and attitudes to bullying. Rates of reported bullying are disturbingly high; they vary with year, gender and school location, partly as a result of opportunities for bullying.
