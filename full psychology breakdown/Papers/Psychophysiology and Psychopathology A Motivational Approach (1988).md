# Psychophysiology and Psychopathology: A Motivational Approach

**Authors:** Don C. Fowles
**Year:** 1988
**Citations:** 564
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1469-8986.1988.tb01873.x

## Abstract

ABSTRACT The current zeitgeist strongly emphasizes genetic and biochemical approaches to psychopathology over psychological and psychobiological approaches. It is argued that a psychobiological model, involving a theory of motivation derived from the animal learning literature, offers an attractive theoretical bridge between neurochemical influences and the phenotypic features of psychiatric disorders. This model involves separate but interactive appetitive and aversive motivational systems that control behavioral activation (appetitive) and inhibition (aversive).
