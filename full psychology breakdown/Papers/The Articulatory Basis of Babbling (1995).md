## Abstract

This article evaluates the "Frames, then Content" hypothesis for speech acquisition, which states that much of the patterning of babbling is a direct result of production of syllabic "Frames" by means of rhythmic mandibular oscillation, with relatively little of the intrasyllabic and intersyllabic "Content" of the syllable-like cycles under mandible-independent control. Analysis was based on a phonetically transcribed corpus of 6,659 utterances of 6 normally developing infants obtained from one-hour weekly audio-recordings over a 4-6 month period.
