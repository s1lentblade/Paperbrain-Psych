# Accuracy in detecting truths and lies: Documenting the “veracity effect”

**Authors:** Timothy R. Levine, Steven A. McCornack
**Year:** 1999
**Citations:** 410
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/03637759909376468

## Abstract

Deception research has consistently shown that accuracy rates tend to be just over fifty percent when accuracy rates are averaged across truthful and deceptive messages and when an equal number of truths and lies are judged. Breaking accuracy rates down by truths and lies, however, leads to a radically different conclusion. Across three studies, a large and consistent veracity effect was evident. Truths are most often correctly identified as honest, but errors predominate when lies are judged. Truth accuracy is substantially greater than chance, but the detection of lies was often significantly below chance.
