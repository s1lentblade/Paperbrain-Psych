# Spectral balance as an acoustic correlate of linguistic stress

**Authors:** Agaath M. C. Sluijter, Vincent J. van Heuven
**Year:** 1996
**Citations:** 546
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1121/1.417955

## Abstract

Although intensity has been reported as a reliable acoustical correlate of stress, it is generally considered a weak cue in the perception of linguistic stress. In natural speech stressed syllables are produced with more vocal effort. It is known that, if a speaker produces more vocal effort, higher frequencies increase more than lower frequencies. In this study, the effects of lexical stress on intensity are examined in the abstraction from the confounding accent variation. A production study was carried out in which ten speakers produced Dutch lexical and reiterant disyllabic minimal stress pairs spoken with and without an accent in a fixed carrier sentence.
