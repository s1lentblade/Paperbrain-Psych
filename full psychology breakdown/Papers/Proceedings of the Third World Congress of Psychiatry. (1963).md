## Abstract

The remarkable Third Congress of Psychiatry held in June, 1961, in Canada is poignantly recaptured in these two volumes. This is the first time that this congress met in the Western hemisphere. The participants and members came from almost every country in the world and represented all orientations and disciplines. One can almost visualize the excitement of that crowded week of International Psychiatry, 1961. The papers in the Proceedings have been grouped under three main headings: the special sessions with subheadings of academic lectures, three leading themes, and a discussion of scientific creativity; the plenary sessions; and general sessions.
