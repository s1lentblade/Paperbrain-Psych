## Abstract

As we begin the new millennium, it is an appropriate time to examine what we have learned about personality‐performance relationships over the past century and to embark on new directions for research. In this study we quantitatively summarize the results of 15 prior meta‐analytic studies that have investigated the relationship between the Five Factor Model (FFM) personality traits and job performance. Results support the previous findings that conscientiousness is a valid predictor across performance measures in all occupations studied.
