# Does social exclusion motivate interpersonal reconnection? Resolving the "porcupine problem."

**Authors:** Jon K. Maner, Mark Schaller
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 1,140
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.92.1.42

## Abstract

Evidence from 6 experiments supports the social reconnection hypothesis, which posits that the experience of social exclusion increases the motivation to forge social bonds with new sources of potential affiliation. Threat of social exclusion led participants to express greater interest in making new friends, to increase their desire to work with others, to form more positive impressions of novel social targets, and to assign greater rewards to new interaction partners. Findings also suggest potential boundary conditions to the social reconnection hypothesis.
