# Affiliate Stigma Among Caregivers of People with Intellectual Disability or Mental Illness

**Authors:** Winnie W. S. Mak, Rebecca Y. M. Cheung
**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 490
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1468-3148.2008.00426.x

## Abstract

Background Affiliate stigma refers to the extent of self‐stigmatization among associates of the targeted minorities. Given previous studies on caregiver stigma were mostly qualitative in nature, a conceptually based, unified, quantitative instrument to measure affiliate stigma is still lacking. Materials and Methods Two hundred and ten caregivers of people with intellectual disability (CPID) and 108 caregivers of people with mental illness (CPMI) were recruited to validate the 22‐item Affiliate Stigma Scale with caregiving stress, subjective burden and positive perceptions.
