**Authors:** Nathan Salmón

## Abstract

The nature of the information content of declarative sentences is a central topic in the philosophy of language. The natural view that a sentence like John loves Mary contains information in which two individuals occur as constituents is termed the naive theory, and is one that has been abandoned by most contemporary scholars. This theory was refuted originally by philosopher Gottlob Frege.
