# The Brain's concepts: the role of the Sensory-motor system in conceptual knowledge

**Authors:** Vittorio Gallese, George Lakoff
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 2,679
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/02643290442000310

## Abstract

Concepts are the elementary units of reason and linguistic meaning. They are conventional and relatively stable. As such, they must somehow be the result of neural activity in the brain. The questions are: Where? and How? A common philosophical position is that all concepts-even concepts about action and perception-are symbolic and abstract, and therefore must be implemented outside the brain's sensory-motor system. We will argue against this position using (1) neuroscientific evidence; (2) results from neural computation; and (3) results about the nature of concepts from cognitive linguistics. We will propose that the sensory-motor system has the right kind of structure to characterise both
