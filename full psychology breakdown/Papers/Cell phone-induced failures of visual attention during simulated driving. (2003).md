# Cell phone-induced failures of visual attention during simulated driving.

**Authors:** David L. Strayer, William Johnston
**Year:** 2003
**Citations:** 1,030
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/1076-898x.9.1.23

## Abstract

This research examined the effects of hands-free cell phone conversations on simulated driving. The authors found that these conversations impaired driver's reactions to vehicles braking in front of them. The authors assessed whether this impairment could be attributed to a withdrawal of attention from the visual scene, yielding a form of inattention blindness. Cell phone conversations impaired explicit recognition memory for roadside billboards. Eye-tracking data indicated that this was due to reduced attention to foveal information.
