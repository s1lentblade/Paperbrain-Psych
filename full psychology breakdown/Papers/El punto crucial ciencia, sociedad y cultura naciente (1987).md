## Abstract

Small-angle scattering of X-rays (SAXS) is a method for the low-resolution structural characterization of biological macromolecules in solution. The technique is highly complementary to the high-resolution methods of X-ray crystallography and NMR. SAXS not only provides shapes, oligomeric state, and quaternary structures of folded proteins and protein complexes but also allows for the quantitative analysis of flexible systems. Here, major procedures are presented to characterize intrinsically disordered proteins (IDPs) using SAXS.
