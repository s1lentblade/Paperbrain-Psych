# Self-Control and Grit

**Authors:** Angela Duckworth, James J. Gross
**Year:** 2014
**Citations:** 966
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/0963721414541462

## Abstract

Other than talent and opportunity, what makes some people more successful than others? One important determinant of success is self-control - the capacity to regulate attention, emotion, and behavior in the presence of temptation. A second important determinant of success is grit - the tenacious pursuit of a dominant superordinate goal despite setbacks. Self-control and grit are strongly correlated, but not perfectly so. This means that some people with high levels of self-control capably handle temptations but do not consistently pursue a dominant goal.
