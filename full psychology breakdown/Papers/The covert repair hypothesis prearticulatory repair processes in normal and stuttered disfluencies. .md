# The covert repair hypothesis: prearticulatory repair processes in normal and stuttered disfluencies.

**Authors:** Albert Postma, Herman Kolk
**Year:** 1993
**Citations:** 354

## Abstract

Self-repairing of speech errors demonstrates that speakers possess a monitoring device with which they verify the correctness of the speech flow. There is substantial evidence that this speech monitor not only comprises an auditory component (i.e., hearing one's own speech), but also an internal part: inspection of the speech program prior to its motoric execution. Errors thus may be detected before they are actually articulated.
