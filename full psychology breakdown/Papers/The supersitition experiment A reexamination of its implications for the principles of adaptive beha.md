# The "supersitition" experiment: A reexamination of its implications for the principles of adaptive behavior.

**Year:** 1971
**Citations:** 1,062

## Abstract

Replication and extension of Skinner's superstition experiment showed the development of two kinds of behavior at asymptote: interim activities (related to adjunctive behavior) occurred just after food delivery; the terminal response (a discriminated operant) occurred toward the end of the interval and continued until food delivery. These data suggest a view of operant conditioning (the terminal response) in terms of two sets of principles: principles of behavioral variation that describe the origins of behavior appropriate to a situation, in advance of reinforcement; and principles of reinforcement that describe the selective elimination of behavior so produced.
