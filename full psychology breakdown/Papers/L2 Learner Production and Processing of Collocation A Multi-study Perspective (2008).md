# L2 Learner Production and Processing of Collocation: A Multi-study Perspective

**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 386
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3138/cmlr.64.3.429

## Abstract

Abstract: This article presents a series of studies focusing on L2 production and processing of adjective–noun collocations (e.g., social services). In Study 1, 810 adjective–noun collocations were extracted from 31 essays written by Russian learners of English. About half of these collocations appeared frequently in the British National Corpus (BNC); one-quarter failed to appear in the BNC at all, while another quarter had a very low BNC frequency. Based on frequency data and mutual information (MI) scores, it was discovered that around 45% of all learner collocations were, in fact, appropriate collocations, that is, frequent and strongly associated English word combinations.
