# Work Values and Job Rewards: A Theory of Job Satisfaction

**Authors:** Arne L. Kalleberg
**Year:** 1977
**Citations:** 1,232
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.2307/2117735

## Abstract

This paper attempts to develop a theory of job satisfaction which incorporates differences in work values and perceived job characteristics as key explanatory variables. It empirically examines the relationship between job satisfaction and the work values and job rewards associated with six dimensions of work-intrinsic, convenience, financial, relations with co-workers, career opportunities and resource adequacy. It is found that work values have independent-effects on job satisfaction. The extent to which workers are able to obtain perceived job rewards is conceptualized to be a function of their degree of control over their employment situations.
