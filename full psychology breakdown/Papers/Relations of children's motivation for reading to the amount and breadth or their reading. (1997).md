# Relations of children's motivation for reading to the amount and breadth or their reading.

**Authors:** Allan Wigfield, John T. Guthrie
**Year:** 1997
**Citations:** 1,298
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-0663.89.3.420

## Abstract

The authors explored different aspects of children's reading motivation and how children's motivation related to the amount and breadth of their reading. The reading motives assessed included self-efficacy, intrinsic-extrinsic motivation and goals, and social aspects. Fourth- and 5th-grade children (N = 105) completed a new reading motivation questionnaire twice during a school year. Children's reading amount and breadth were measured using diaries and questionnaires. Children's reading motivation was found to be multidimensional.
