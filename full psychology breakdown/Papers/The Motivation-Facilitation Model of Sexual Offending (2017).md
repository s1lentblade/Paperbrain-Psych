# The Motivation-Facilitation Model of Sexual Offending

**Authors:** Michael C. Seto
**Year:** 2017
**Citations:** 313
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/1079063217720919

## Abstract

In this article, I describe the motivation-facilitation model of sexual offending, which identifies the traits of paraphilia, high sex drive, and intense mating effort as primary motivations for sexual offenses, as well as trait (e.g., antisocial personality) and state (e.g., intoxication) factors that can facilitate acting on these motivations when opportunities exist. Originally developed to explain contact sexual offending against children, the motivation-facilitation model was subsequently extended as an explanation for child pornography offending and for online solicitations of young adolescents.
