**Year:** 2006
**Citations:** 1,531

## Abstract

Teachers with an autonomy-supportive style rely on different instructional behaviors to motivate their students than do teachers with a controlling style. In the present investigation, the authors tested which of these instructional behaviors actually correlated positively or negatively with students ’ autonomy. The authors used Deci, Spiegel, Ryan, Koestner, &amp;amp; Kauffman’s (1982) teacher–student laboratory paradigm to randomly assign 72 pairs of same-sex preservice teachers into the role of either teacher or student.
