# Recognizing Spoken Words: The Neighborhood Activation Model

**Authors:** Paul A. Luce, David B. Pisoni
**Year:** 1998
**Citations:** 1,922
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/00003446-199802000-00001

## Abstract

The results of each of these experiments demonstrated that the number and nature of words in a similarity neighborhood affect the speed and accuracy of word recognition. A neighborhood probability rule was developed that adequately predicted identification performance. This rule, based on Luce's (1959) choice rule, combines stimulus word intelligibility, neighborhood confusability, and frequency into a single expression. Based on this rule, a model of auditory word recognition, the neighborhood activation model, was proposed.
