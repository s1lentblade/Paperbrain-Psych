## Abstract

A body‐accessibility questionnaire was submitted to 168 male and 140 female unmarried college students. The aim was to determine the extent to which college students permit their parents and closest friends of each sex to see and touch their bodies, and the extent to which they have seen and touched the bodies of these ‘target‐persons’. The bodies of the S s and of the target‐persons were reportedly more visually than tactually accessible. The greatest range of tactual interchange occurred between the S s and their closest friends of the opposite sex.
