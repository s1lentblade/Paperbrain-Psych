# The Impact of Children with High-Functioning Autism on Parental Stress, Sibling Adjustment, and Family Functioning

**Authors:** Patricia A. Rao, Déborah C. Beidel
**Year:** 2009
**Citations:** 353
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/0145445509336427

## Abstract

The article discusses a study conducted to investigate the impact of children with high-functioning autism (HFA) on parental stress, sibling adjustment, and family functioning; the study involves a sample of parents of 15 children with HFA and parents of 15 matched control children who completed questionnaires measuring the dependent variables. The results indicate parents of children with HFA experience significantly more parenting stress than parents of children with no psychological disorder, which was found to be directly related to characteristics of the children.
