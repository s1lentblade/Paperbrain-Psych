## Abstract

The assumption that human behavior is largely under conscious con trol has taken a theoretical battering in recent years. Although this assault in some ways resembles the previous century's Freudian revolution, there are important differences between the two. Freud's views of unconscious mechanisms were embedded in a theory that never achieved conclusive support among scientists, despite many empirical theory-testing efforts in the middle third of the twentieth century.1 Consequently, most psycholo gists have abandoned Freud's psychoanalytic theory of unconscious mental processes.
