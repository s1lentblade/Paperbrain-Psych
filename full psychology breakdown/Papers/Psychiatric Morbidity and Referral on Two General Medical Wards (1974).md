# Psychiatric Morbidity and Referral on Two General Medical Wards

**Authors:** G. P. Maguire, John Bancroft
**Year:** 1974
**Citations:** 264
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1136/bmj.1.5902.268

## Abstract

Psychiatric morbidity among 230 medical inpatients was determined by a two-stage screening procedure, using the General Health Questionnaire and Standardized Psychiatric Interview. Of these patients, 23% were considered psychiatrically ill, affective disorders being the commonest illnesses encountered; and 27 (12%) were psychiatrically referred. While referral was related to severity of psychiatric illness and previous psychiatric illness, the degree to which the psychiatric illness obtruded or created problems in management appeared more crucial in determining referral.
