**Year:** 1989
**Citations:** 332

## Abstract

Since its publication in 1989, Console and Classify has become a classic work in the history of science and in French intellectual history. Now with a new afterword, this much-cited and much-discussed book gives readers the chance to revisit the rise of psychiatry in nineteenth-century France, the shape it took and why, and its importance both then and in contemporary society. Goldstein has raised our understanding of the politics of psychiatric professionalization on to a new plane.-Roy Porter, Times Higher Education Supplement [A]n historiographical tour de force, quite simply the most insightful work on the subject in English or any other language. . . .
