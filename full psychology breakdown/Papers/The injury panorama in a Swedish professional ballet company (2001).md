# The injury panorama in a Swedish professional ballet company

**Authors:** Charlotte Juul Nilsson, Lars‐Erik Strender
**Year:** 2001
**Citations:** 244
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s001670100195

## Abstract

We performed a combined retro- and prospective study of injuries in a Swedish professional, classical ballet company during 5 consecutive years. There were 390 injuries incurred by 98 dancers over a 5-year period, i.e., 0.6 injuries/1000 dance hours. Most injuries were considered to be due to overuse. The median sick leave was 2.3 weeks per injury. The foot and ankle region is vulnerable in classical ballet dancers, and overuse injuries can result in long periods of sick leave. Of the dancers employed for more than 1 year 95% were suffered injuries during the study period.
