## Abstract

Understanding the factors which influence the processing of information seems fundamental for the clinical psychiatrist and behavioral scientist. Practical problems stemming from information overload, underload, and off-balance load are seen daily in the hospital and clinic. From the postoperative patient isolated, enfeebled, subjected to the monotony of respirator and cardiac monitor to the sensitive child bombarded with conflicting instructions and competing expectations, information dys-load merits specific consideration.
