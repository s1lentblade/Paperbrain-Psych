# Effects of keyboard tray geometry on upper body posture and comfort

**Authors:** Alan Hedge, Daniel McCrobie
**Year:** 1999
**Citations:** 288
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/001401399184983

## Abstract

The effects of a downward-tilting (DT) keyboard tray on wrist posture, seated posture and self-assessed musculoskeletal discomfort were investigated in a field experiment. Thirty-eight professional office workers were studied. A pretest assessed how they typed using either a conventional keyboard on a desk or on an articulating keyboard tray, and with or without wrist rests. Workers were randomly allocated to a control (n = 15) or test group (n = 23) that used their existing keyboard in a DT system. A post-test was conducted 3 weeks later.
