# Personal Being: A Theory for Individual Psychology

**Authors:** Rom Harré
**Year:** 1984
**Citations:** 596

## Abstract

The undoubted fact of human individuality has remained outside the field of interest of scientific psychology. Neither the central organization of consciousness nor individual powers of action have been dealt with in substantial research programmes. Yet every facet of our mental lives is influenced by how our minds are organized. How much of this organization comes from the languages and social practices of the cultures into which we are born is undetermined. In this book, Rom Harre explores the radical thesis that most of our personal being may be of social origin.
