# Personality and leadership: A qualitative and quantitative review.

**Authors:** Timothy A. Judge, Megan W. Gerhardt
**Year:** 2002
**Citations:** 2,953
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0021-9010.87.4.765

## Abstract

This article provides a qualitative review of the trait perspective in leadership research, followed by a meta-analysis. The authors used the five-factor model as an organizing framework and meta-analyzed 222 correlations from 73 samples. Overall, the correlations with leadership were Neuroticism = -.24, Extraversion = .31, Openness to Experience = .24, Agreeableness = .08, and Conscientiousness = .28. Results indicated that the relations of Neuroticism, Extraversion, Openness to Experience, and Conscientiousness with leadership generalized in that more than 90% of the individual correlations were greater than 0.
