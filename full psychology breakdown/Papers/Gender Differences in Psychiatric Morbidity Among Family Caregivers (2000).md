# Gender Differences in Psychiatric Morbidity Among Family Caregivers

**Authors:** Jennifer L. Yee, Richard Schulz
**Year:** 2000
**Citations:** 659

## Abstract

The major goal of this article was to review and synthesize the empirical research on caregiver gender and psychiatric morbidity, with the aim of answering three questions: (a) Is there greater psychiatric morbidity among female than male caregivers, (b) is the excess psychiatric morbidity among female caregivers attributable to caregiving, and (c) what factors in the caregiving situation contribute to the excess psychiatric morbidity among female caregivers? In almost all studies reviewed, women caregivers reported more psychiatric symptoms than men caregivers.
