# Central Oxytocin Administration Reduces Stress-Induced Corticosterone Release and Anxiety Behavior in Rats<sup>1</sup>

**Authors:** Richard Windle, C.D. Ingram
**Year:** 1997
**Citations:** 658

## Abstract

Endocrine responses to noise stress and anxiety-related behaviors were measured in groups of ovariectomized, estradiol-treated female rats given central infusions of oxytocin. Control animals receiving isotonic saline showed a large increase in plasma corticosterone concentrations in response to 10 min of white noise. This response to noise stress was significantly and dose dependently decreased by oxytocin administered intracerebroventricularly at 10 or 100 ng/h for 5 days. Oxytocin also significantly decreased rearing behavior during this stress.
