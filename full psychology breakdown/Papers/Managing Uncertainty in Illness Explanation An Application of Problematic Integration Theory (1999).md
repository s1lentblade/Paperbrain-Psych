## Abstract

Case One. The parents of a newborn observe that their daughter’s pupils are different sizes. A pediatric opthamologist diagnoses her condition as Horner’s syndrome. Although the syndrome has no effects of its own, it may be symptomatic of a brain stem tumor, lung cancer, or a traumatic head injury. The physician recommends a CAT scan to explore these possibilities. The parents carefully investigate the desirability of this test.
