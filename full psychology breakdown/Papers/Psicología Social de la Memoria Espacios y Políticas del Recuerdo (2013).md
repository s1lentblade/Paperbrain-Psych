## Abstract

The aim of the social psychology of memory is to understand and intervene in the processes of collective memory from a discursive and performative approach, looking both at the narrations about the past and at the uses of public space in the actions of recalling. This paper presents the conceptual path that the Social Psychology of Memory Team at the University of Chile has developed during its research and action trajectory. Drawing on the multiple studies conducted, this paper shows how incorporating the performative dimension of memory has involved a theoretical and methodological emphasis on the study of commemoration, places of memory and policies on memories.
