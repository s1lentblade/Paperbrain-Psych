# Thoughts beyond words: When language overshadows insight.

**Authors:** Jonathan W. Schooler, Kevin R. Brooks
**Year:** 1993
**Citations:** 659
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0096-3445.122.2.166

## Abstract

Four experiments examined whether verbalization can interfere with insight problem solving. In Experiment 1, Ss were interrupted during problem solving and asked either to verbalize their strategies (retrospective verbalization) or engage in an unrelated activity (control). Ss in the retrospective verbalization condition were significantly less successful than control subjects at solving the problems. Experiment 2 replicated the finding of Experiment 1 and demonstrated that the control Ss' advantage was not due to any beneficial effect of the interruption. In Experiment 3, concurrent, nondirective verbalization impaired the solving of insight problems but had no effect on noninsight problems
