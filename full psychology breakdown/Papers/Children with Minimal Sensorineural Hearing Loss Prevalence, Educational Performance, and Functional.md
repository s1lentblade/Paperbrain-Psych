# Children with Minimal Sensorineural Hearing Loss: Prevalence, Educational Performance, and Functional Status

**Authors:** Fred H. Bess, Robert A. Parker
**Year:** 1998
**Citations:** 839
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/00003446-199810000-00001

## Abstract

The prevalence of hearing loss in the schools almost doubles when children with MSHL are included. This large, education-based study shows clinically important associations between MSHL and school behavior and performance. Children with MSHL experienced more difficulty than normally hearing children on a series of educational and functional test measures. Although additional research is necessary, results suggest the need for audiologists, speech-language pathologists, and educators to evaluate carefully our identification and management approaches with this population.
