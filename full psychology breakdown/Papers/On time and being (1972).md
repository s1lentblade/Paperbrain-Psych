# On time and being

**Authors:** Martín Heidegger
**Year:** 1972
**Citations:** 324

## Abstract

On and charts so-called turn in Martin Heidegger's philosophy away from his earlier metaphysics in and to his later thoughts after the end of philosophy. title lecture, Time and Being, shows how Heidegger reconceived both Being and time, introducing new concept of the event of Appropriation to help give his metaphysical ideas nonmetaphysical meanings. On and also contains a summary of six seminar sessions that Heidegger conducted on Time and Being, a lecture called The End of Philosophy and Task of Thinking, and an autobiographical sketch of Heidegger's intellectual history in My Way of Phenomenology.
