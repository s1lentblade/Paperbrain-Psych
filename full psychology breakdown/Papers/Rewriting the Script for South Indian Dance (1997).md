## Abstract

For the past several years I have been investigating the local circumstances of what is commonly referred to as a of dance in South India in the 1930s, working outward from a study of the padam genre of dance music.' Reading the work of Jennifer Post (1989), Regula Qureshi (1991), and Tapati Guha-Thakurta (1992), which describes and theorizes revivals in the performing and visual arts in the northern part of the subcontinent, has convinced me that the events in South India bear study as part of a larger pattern.
