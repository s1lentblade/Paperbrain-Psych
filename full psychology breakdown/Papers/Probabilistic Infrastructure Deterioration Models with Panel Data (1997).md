## Abstract

Statistical models of infrastructure facility deterioration are typically estimated using panel data sets of in-service facilities. For example, biannual ratings of bridges have been used to develop discrete models of component deterioration by a number of researchers. Unfortunately, these models have not accounted for the presence of heterogeneity in the panel data, which may lead to biased coefficient estimates. Furthermore, researchers have usually imposed a Markovian specification in the development of such models, implying that the probabilistic deterioration in a given period is independent of history.
