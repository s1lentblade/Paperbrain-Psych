# Understanding, compliance and psychological impact of the SARS quarantine experience

**Authors:** D L Reynolds, Rima Styra
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 933
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1017/s0950268807009156

## Abstract

This study examines a cohort of persons quarantined during the 2003 SARS outbreak in Canada and describes their understanding of, difficulties and compliance with, and the psychological impact of the quarantine experience. A mailed questionnaire was administered to 1912 eligible adults and included the Impact of Events Scale - Revised (IES-R) to assess symptoms of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Self-reported compliance with all required quarantine measures was low (15.8+/-2.3%), although significantly higher when the rationale for quarantine was understood (P=0.018).
