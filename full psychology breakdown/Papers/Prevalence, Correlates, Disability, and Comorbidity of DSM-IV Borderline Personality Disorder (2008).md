# Prevalence, Correlates, Disability, and Comorbidity of DSM-IV Borderline Personality Disorder

**Authors:** Bridget F. Grant, W. June Ruan
**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 1,602
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.4088/jcp.v69n0404

## Abstract

BPD is much more prevalent in the general population than previously recognized, is equally prevalent among men and women, and is associated with considerable mental and physical disability, especially among women. Unique and common factors may differentially contribute to disorder-specific comorbidity with BPD, and some of these associations appear to be sex-specific. There is a need for future epidemiologic, clinical, and genetically informed studies to identify unique and common factors that underlie disorder-specific comorbidity with BPD. Important sex differences observed in rates of BPD and associations with BPD can inform more focused, hypothesis-driven investigations of these factors
