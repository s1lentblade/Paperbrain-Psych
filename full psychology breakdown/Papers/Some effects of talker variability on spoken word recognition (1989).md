# Some effects of talker variability on spoken word recognition

**Authors:** John W. Mullennix, Christopher S. Martin
**Year:** 1989
**Citations:** 535
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1121/1.397688

## Abstract

The perceptual consequences of trial-to-trial changes in the voice of the talker on spoken word recognition were examined. The results from a series of experiments using perceptual identification and naming tasks demonstrated that perceptual performance decreases when the voice of the talker changes from trial to trial compared to performance when the voice on each trial remains the same. In addition, the effects of talker variability on word recognition appeared to be more robust and less dependent on task than the effects of word frequency and lexical structure.
