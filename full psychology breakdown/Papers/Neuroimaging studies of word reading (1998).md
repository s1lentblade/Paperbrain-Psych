# Neuroimaging studies of word reading

**Authors:** Julie A. Fiez, Steven E. Petersen
**Year:** 1998
**Citations:** 728
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.95.3.914

## Abstract

This review discusses how neuroimaging can contribute to our understanding of a fundamental aspect of skilled reading: the ability to pronounce a visually presented word. One contribution of neuroimaging is that it provides a tool for localizing brain regions that are active during word reading. To assess the extent to which similar results are obtained across studies, a quantitative review of nine neuroimaging investigations of word reading was conducted.
