# Randomised controlled trial of non-directive counselling, cognitive-behaviour therapy, and usual general practitioner care for patients with depression. I: Clinical effectiveness

**Authors:** E. S. Ward, Julia Addington‐Hall
**Year:** 2000
**Citations:** 245
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1136/bmj.321.7273.1383

## Abstract

Psychological therapy was a more effective treatment for depression than usual general practitioner care in the short term, but after one year there was no difference in outcome.
