# The concept of boundaries in clinical practice: theoretical and risk- management dimensions

**Authors:** Thomas G. Gutheil, Glen O. Gabbard
**Year:** 1993
**Citations:** 579
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1176/ajp.150.2.188

## Abstract

The authors systematically examine the concept of boundaries and boundary violations in clinical practice, particularly as they relate to recent sexual misconduct litigation. They selectively review the literature on the subject and identify critical areas that require explication in terms of harmful versus nonharmful boundary issues short of sexual misconduct. These areas include role; time; place and space; money; gifts, services, and related matters; clothing; language; self-disclosure and related matters; and physical contact.
