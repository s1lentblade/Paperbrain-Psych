# Preventing Child Abuse and Neglect: A Randomized Trial of Nurse Home Visitation

**Authors:** David L. Olds, Robert Tatelbaum
**Year:** 1986
**Citations:** 947
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1542/peds.78.1.65

## Abstract

A program of prenatal and infancy home visitation by nurses was tested as a method of preventing a wide range of health and developmental problems in children born to primiparas who were either teenagers, unmarried, or of low socioeconomic status. Among the women at highest risk for care-giving dysfunction, those who were visited by a nurse had fewer instances of verified child abuse and neglect during the first 2 years of their children's lives (P = .07); they were observed in their homes to restrict and punish their children less frequently, and they provided more appropriate play materials; their babies were seen in the emergency room less frequently during the first year of life.
