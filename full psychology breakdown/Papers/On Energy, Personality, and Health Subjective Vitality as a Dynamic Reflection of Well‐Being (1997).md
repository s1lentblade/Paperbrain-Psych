# On Energy, Personality, and Health: Subjective Vitality as a Dynamic Reflection of Well‐Being

**Authors:** Richard M. Ryan, Christina M. Frederick
**Year:** 1997
**Citations:** 2,691
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-6494.1997.tb00326.x

## Abstract

In this article, we examine subjective vitality, a positive feeling of aliveness and energy, in six studies. Subjective vitality is hypothesized to reflect organismic well-being and thus should covary with both psychological and somatic factors that impact the energy available to the self. Associations are shown between subjective vitality and several indexes of psychological well-being; somatic factors such as physical symptoms and perceived body functioning; and basic personality traits and affective dispositions.
