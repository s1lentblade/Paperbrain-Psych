## Abstract

The development of the Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI) itself has had a major impact on the shape and direction of burnout research. The availability of an easily administered, self-report measure was an attractive incentive to many researchers, who then incorporated the measure into their studies. In doing so, they accepted, at least implicitly, the multidimensional model on which the MBI was based. Regardless of the specific form, a multidimensional model has some important implications for interventions.
