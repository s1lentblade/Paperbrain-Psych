# Older Consumers' Disposition of Special Possessions

**Authors:** Linda L. Price, Carolyn Folkman Curasi
**Year:** 2000
**Citations:** 578
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1086/314319

## Abstract

This article explores precipitating events, emotions, and decisions associated with older consumers' disposition of special possessions. Findings are based on analyses of semistructured interviews with 80 older consumers, complemented by depth interviews with seven informants. Cherished possessions and their disposition play a significant role in older consumers' reminiscence and life review. Concerns about disposition of special possessions involve strong and ambivalent emotions. Older consumers voice concern over avoiding intrafamilial conflict, reducing uncertainty, and exercising control over the future life of special possessions.
