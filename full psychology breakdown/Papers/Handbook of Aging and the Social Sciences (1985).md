## Abstract

All of those interested in the problems of aging will welcome the appearance of the second edition of the series<i>The Handbooks of Aging</i>. The other two books in the series cover the biology and psychology of aging. This handbook is specifically designed to complement, but not to duplicate, the work of the first edition. It offers a treasure trove of information about the elderly and a vivid demonstration of what the social sciences can offer to our understanding of aging. Carefully crafted and well designed, it serves as an invaluable reference. Each of the chapters is well documented and provides ready access to the literature on the subject covered.
