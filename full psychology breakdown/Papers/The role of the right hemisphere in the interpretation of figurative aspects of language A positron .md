# The role of the right hemisphere in the interpretation of figurative aspects of language A positron emission tomography activation study

**Authors:** Gabriella Bottini, Dona A. Frith
**Year:** 1994
**Citations:** 668
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/brain/117.6.1241

## Abstract

We investigated cerebral activity in six normal volunteers using PET to explore the hypothesis that the right hemisphere has a specific role in the interpretation of figurative aspects of language such as metaphors. We also mapped the anatomical structures involved in sentence comprehension. During regional cerebral blood flow measurement subjects were asked to perform three different linguistic tasks: (i) metaphorical comprehension; (ii) literal comprehension of sentences; and (iii) a lexical-decision task.
