# From conceptual roles to structural relations: Bridging the syntactic cleft.

**Authors:** Kathryn Bock, Randal Morey
**Year:** 1992
**Citations:** 626

## Abstract

The distinction between underlying and superficial linguistic structure is a staple of modern cognitive psychology. Despite increasingly diverse conceptions of syntactic relations in linguistic theory, the received view in psycholinguistics has remained one in which the entities assigned to underlying relations may assume different surface relations.
