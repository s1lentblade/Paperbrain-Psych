# Nonword Repetition and Child Language Impairment

**Authors:** Chris Dollaghan, Thomas F. Campbell
**Year:** 1998
**Citations:** 995
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1044/jslhr.4105.1136

## Abstract

A brief, processing-dependent, nonword repetition task, designed to minimize biases associated with traditional language tests, was investigated. In Study 1, no overlap in nonword repetition performance was found between a group of 20 school-age children enrolled in language intervention (LI) and a group of 20 age-matched peers developing language normally (LN). In Study 2, a comparison of likelihood ratios for the nonword repetition task and for a traditional language test revealed that nonword repetition distinguished between children independently identified as LI and LN with a high degree of accuracy, by contrast with the traditional language test.
