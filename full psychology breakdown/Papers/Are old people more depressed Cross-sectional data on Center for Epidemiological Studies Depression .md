# Are old people more depressed? Cross-sectional data on Center for Epidemiological Studies Depression Scale factors.

**Authors:** Margaret Gatz, Margo‐Lea Hurwicz
**Year:** 1990
**Citations:** 236
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0882-7974.5.2.284

## Abstract

Age differences on the 20-item Center for Epidemiological Studies Depression Scale (CES-D) were examined for 4 age-cohort groups: 20-39 years (n = 548), 40-54 years (n = 218), 55-69 years (n = 352), and 70-98 years (n = 212). On total CES-D, there was a significant age effect and quadratic trend, with means for the middle aged least and those for the oldest most elevated. On 4 CES-D subscales--Depressed Mood, Psychomotor Retardation, Lack of Well-being (i.e., reverse-scored items), and Interpersonal Difficulties--the oldest group scored highest only on lack of well-being.
