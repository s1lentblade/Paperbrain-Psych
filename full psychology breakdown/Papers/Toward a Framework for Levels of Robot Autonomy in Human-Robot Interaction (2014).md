# Toward a Framework for Levels of Robot Autonomy in Human-Robot Interaction

**Year:** 2014
**Citations:** 566
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5898/jhri.3.2.beer

## Abstract

A critical construct related to human-robot interaction (HRI) is autonomy, which varies widely across robot platforms. Levels of robot autonomy (LORA), ranging from teleoperation to fully autonomous systems, influence the way in which humans and robots may interact with one another. Thus, there is a need to understand HRI by identifying variables that influence - and are influenced by - robot autonomy. Our overarching goal is to develop a framework for levels of robot autonomy in HRI. To reach this goal, the framework draws links between HRI and human-automation interaction, a field with a long history of studying and understanding human-related variables.
