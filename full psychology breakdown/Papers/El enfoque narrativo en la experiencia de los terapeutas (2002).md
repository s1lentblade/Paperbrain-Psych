## Abstract

The viability and morphologic changes of intraperitoneal free cancer cells in advanced gastric cancer patients were examined by Giemsa and enzymologic staining and by tritiated thymidine uptake. Although many free cancer cells in the pouch of Douglas showed moderate degeneration, viable, morphologically intact cells were noted, leading to the possibility of their implantation and proliferation in the peritoneum. Serosal cancer cells showed a high degree of viability.
