# Indochinese versions of the Hopkins Symptom Checklist-25: a screening instrument for the psychiatric care of refugees

**Year:** 1987
**Citations:** 709
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1176/ajp.144.4.497

## Abstract

The United States is accepting the largest number of displaced persons since World War II. Over 70% are Southeast Asians; many have suffered serious war trauma and torture. Cultural differences in health-seeking behavior and lack of specialized mental health services make the recognition of psychiatric distress in Southeast Asia refugees difficult for American health care providers. The authors describe the development and validation of Cambodian, Laotian, and Vietnamese versions of the Hopkins Symptom Checklist-25.
