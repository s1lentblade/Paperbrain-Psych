# Diagnostic criteria for complicated grief disorder

**Authors:** Mardi J. Horowitz, Charles Stinson
**Year:** 1997
**Citations:** 644
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1176/ajp.154.7.904

## Abstract

A new diagnosis of complicated grief disorder may be indicated. Its criteria would include the current experience (more than a year after a loss) of intense intrusive thoughts, pangs of severe emotion, distressing yearnings, feeling excessively alone and empty, excessively avoiding tasks reminiscent of the deceased, unusual sleep disturbances, and maladaptive levels of loss of interest in personal activities.
