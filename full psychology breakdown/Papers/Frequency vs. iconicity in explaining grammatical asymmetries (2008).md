# Frequency vs. iconicity in explaining grammatical asymmetries

**Authors:** Martín Haspelmath
**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 578
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1515/cog.2008.001

## Abstract

This paper argues that three widely accepted motivating factors subsumed under the broad heading of iconicity, namely iconicity of quantity, iconicity of complexity and iconicity of cohesion, in fact have no role in explaining grammatical asymmetries and should be discarded. The iconicity accounts of the relevant phenomena have been proposed by authorities like Jakobson, Haiman and Givón, but I argue that these linguists did not sufficiently consider alternative usage-based explanations in terms of frequency of use.
