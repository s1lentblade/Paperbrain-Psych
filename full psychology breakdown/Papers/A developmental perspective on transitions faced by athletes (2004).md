# A developmental perspective on transitions faced by athletes

**Authors:** Paul Wylleman, David Lavallee
**Year:** 2004
**Citations:** 473

## Abstract

507 To reach and to remain at the elite level in competitive sport require athletes—and those around them—to “invest” at different levels (e.g., physical, social, financial) during a long period of time. The termination of such a high-level athletic career can, therefore, be an emotional experience, not only for the athletes themselves but also for those in their close environment (e.g., parents, children, friends).
