# The Prosocial Classroom: Teacher Social and Emotional Competence in Relation to Student and Classroom Outcomes

**Authors:** Patricia A. Jennings, Mark T. Greenberg
**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 3,366
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3102/0034654308325693

## Abstract

The authors propose a model of the prosocial classroom that highlights the importance of teachers’ social and emotional competence (SEC) and well-being in the development and maintenance of supportive teacher–student relationships, effective classroom management, and successful social and emotional learning program implementation. This model proposes that these factors contribute to creating a classroom climate that is more conducive to learning and that promotes positive developmental outcomes among students.
