# The Masterpiece of Nature: The Evolution and Genetics of Sexuality

**Authors:** Graham Bell
**Year:** 1981
**Citations:** 1,700

## Abstract

Originally published in 1982, The Masterpiece of Nature examines sex as representative of the most important challenge to the modern theory of evolution. The book suggests that sex evolved, not as the result of normal Darwinian processes of natural selection, but through competition between populations or species - a hypothesis elsewhere almost universally discredited. The book also discusses the nature of sex and its consequences for the individual and for the population, as well as various other theories of sex.
