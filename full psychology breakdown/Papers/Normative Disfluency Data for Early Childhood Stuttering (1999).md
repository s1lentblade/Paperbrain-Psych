# Normative Disfluency Data for Early Childhood Stuttering

**Authors:** Nicoline Ambrose, Ehud Yairi
**Year:** 1999
**Citations:** 334
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1044/jslhr.4204.895

## Abstract

Although the past 50 years of research on early childhood stuttering and normal disfluency have produced vital information on the general features of disfluent speech behavior of young children, an adequate normative reference for early stuttering does not exist. The purpose of this report is to provide such reference and to provide a basis for clinical needs of differential diagnosis of stuttering from normal disfluency. Data are presented from 90 stuttering children ages 2 to 5 within 6 months of stuttering onset and from 54 age-matched normally fluent children. Means for disfluency types are presented.
