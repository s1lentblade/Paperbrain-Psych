## Abstract

American Sign Language (ASL) is a fully grammaticized language, not a pantomimic communication system, and it displays the various grammatical characteristics typically found in spoken languages of the world, despite the apparent potential for a different type of organization offered by the visual-gestural modalities. Unlike English, ASL is a morphologically complex language, perhaps most comparable to polysynthetic spoken languages.
