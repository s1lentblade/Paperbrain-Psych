# Depression in sleep disturbance: A review on a bidirectional relationship, mechanisms and treatment

**Authors:** Hong Fang, Anwen Shao
**Year:** 2019
**Citations:** 1,094
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/jcmm.14170

## Abstract

Sleep disturbance is the most prominent symptom in depressive patients and was formerly regarded as a main secondary manifestation of depression. However, many longitudinal studies have identified insomnia as an independent risk factor for the development of emerging or recurrent depression among young, middle-aged and older adults. This bidirectional association between sleep disturbance and depression has created a new perspective that sleep problems are no longer an epiphenomenon of depression but a predictive prodromal symptom. In this review, we highlight the treatment of sleep disturbance before, during and after depression, which probably plays an important role in improving outcomes
