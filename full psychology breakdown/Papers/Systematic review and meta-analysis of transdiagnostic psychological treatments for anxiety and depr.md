# Systematic review and meta-analysis of transdiagnostic psychological treatments for anxiety and depressive disorders in adulthood

**Authors:** Jill M. Newby, Tim Dalgleish
**Year:** 2015
**Citations:** 558
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cpr.2015.06.002

## Abstract

A broad array of transdiagnostic psychological treatments for depressive and anxiety disorders have been evaluated, but existing reviews of this literature are restricted to face-to-face cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) protocols. The current meta-analysis focused on studies evaluating clinician-guided internet/computerised or face-to-face manualised transdiagnostic treatments, to examine their effects on anxiety, depression and quality of life (QOL). Results from 50 studies showed that transdiagnostic treatments are efficacious, with large overall mean uncontrolled effects (pre- to post-treatment) for anxiety and depression (gs=.85 and .91 respectively), and medium for QOL (g=.69).
