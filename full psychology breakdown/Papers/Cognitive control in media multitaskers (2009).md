# Cognitive control in media multitaskers

**Authors:** Eyal Ophir, Clifford Nass, Anthony D. Wagner
**Year:** 2009
**Citations:** 1,683
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0903620106

## Abstract

Chronic media multitasking is quickly becoming ubiquitous, although processing multiple incoming streams of information is considered a challenge for human cognition. A series of experiments addressed whether there are systematic differences in information processing styles between chronically heavy and light media multitaskers. A trait media multitasking index was developed to identify groups of heavy and light media multitaskers. These two groups were then compared along established cognitive control dimensions. Results showed that heavy media multitaskers are more susceptible to interference from irrelevant environmental stimuli and from irrelevant representations in memory.
