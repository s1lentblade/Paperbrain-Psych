**Authors:** David M. Halperin
**Year:** 2007

## Abstract

This work is a crucial effort to understand gay men's relation to sex and risk without recourse to tainted psychological concepts. How we can talk about sex and risk in the age of barebacking - or condomless sex - without invoking the usual bogus and punitive cliches about gay men's alleged low self-esteem, lack of self-control, and other psychological deficits? Are there queer alternatives to psychology for thinking about the inner life of homosexuality? What Do Gay Men Want? explores some of the possibilities.
