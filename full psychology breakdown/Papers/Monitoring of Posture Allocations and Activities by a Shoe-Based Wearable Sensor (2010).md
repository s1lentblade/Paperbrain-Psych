# Monitoring of Posture Allocations and Activities by a Shoe-Based Wearable Sensor

**Authors:** Edward Sazonov, R. Browning
**Year:** 2010
**Citations:** 238

## Abstract

Monitoring of posture allocations and activities enables accurate estimation of energy expenditure and may aid in obesity prevention and treatment. At present, accurate devices rely on multiple sensors distributed on the body and thus may be too obtrusive for everyday use. This paper presents a novel wearable sensor, which is capable of very accurate recognition of common postures and activities. The patterns of heel acceleration and plantar pressure uniquely characterize postures and typical activities while requiring minimal preprocessing and no feature extraction.
