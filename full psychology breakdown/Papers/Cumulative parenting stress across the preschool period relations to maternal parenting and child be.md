# Cumulative parenting stress across the preschool period: relations to maternal parenting and child behaviour at age 5

**Authors:** Keith A. Crnic, Casey Hoffman
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 839

## Abstract

Despite increasing interest in the effects of parenting stress on children and families, many questions remain regarding the nature of parenting stress and the mechanism through which stress exerts its influence across time. In this study, cumulative parenting stress was assessed across the preschool period in a sample of 125 typically developing children and their mothers. Indices of parenting stress included both major life events stress-assessed annually from age 3 to 5, and parenting daily hassles assessed every 6 months across the same period.
