# The nature of procrastination: A meta-analytic and theoretical review of quintessential self-regulatory failure.

**Authors:** Piers Steel
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 3,180
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.133.1.65

## Abstract

Procrastination is a prevalent and pernicious form of self-regulatory failure that is not entirely understood. Hence, the relevant conceptual, theoretical, and empirical work is reviewed, drawing upon correlational, experimental, and qualitative findings. A meta-analysis of procrastination's possible causes and effects, based on 691 correlations, reveals that neuroticism, rebelliousness, and sensation seeking show only a weak connection. Strong and consistent predictors of procrastination were task aversiveness, task delay, self-efficacy, and impulsiveness, as well as conscientiousness and its facets of self-control, distractibility, organization, and achievement motivation.
