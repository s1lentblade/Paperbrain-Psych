## Abstract

Background: Depressive disorders (TD) is a set of prevalent diseases that require proper diagnosis and treatment. In Chile, the instruments of evaluation and diagnosis are scarce. As a way to address this diffi culty, the objective of this study was to validate the Spanish version of the self-reported Patient Health Questionarie (PHQ-9), the consulting population in primary care in Chile. The PHQ-9 to detect depressive symptoms as mild, moderate or severe, and has proven to be an effi cient diagnostic tool.
