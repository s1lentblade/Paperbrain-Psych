**Authors:** Jesús Sanz, Carmelo Vázquez
**Year:** 1998
**Citations:** 235

## Abstract

Reliability, validity, and normative data of the Beck Depression Inventory. This study analyzes the utility of the 1978 version of the Beck Depression Inventory (BDI; Beck, Rush, Shaw y Emery, 1979) as an instrument to identify subclinically depressive subjects in studies intended to validate models of depression. The data were obtained with a sample of 1393 university students. Reliability was high: a) the coefficient alpha was 0.83 and b) test-retest correlations ranged from 0.60 to 0.72 for three different subgroups of the total sample.
