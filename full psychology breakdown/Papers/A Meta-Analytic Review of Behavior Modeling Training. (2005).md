# A Meta-Analytic Review of Behavior Modeling Training.

**Authors:** Paul Taylor, Daniel W. L. Chan
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 497

## Abstract

A meta-analysis of 117 studies evaluated the effects of behavior modeling training (BMT) on 6 training outcomes, across characteristics of training design. BMT effects were largest for learning outcomes, smaller for job behavior, and smaller still for results outcomes. Although BMT effects on declarative knowledge decayed over time, training effects on skills and job behavior remained stable or even increased. Skill development was greatest when learning points were used and presented as rule codes and when training time was longest.
