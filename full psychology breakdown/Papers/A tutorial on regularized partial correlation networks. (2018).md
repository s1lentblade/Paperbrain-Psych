# A tutorial on regularized partial correlation networks.

**Authors:** Sacha Epskamp, Eiko I. Fried
**Year:** 2018
**Citations:** 2,601
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/met0000167

## Abstract

Recent years have seen an emergence of network modeling applied to moods, attitudes, and problems in the realm of psychology. In this framework, psychological variables are understood to directly affect each other rather than being caused by an unobserved latent entity. In this tutorial, we introduce the reader to estimating the most popular network model for psychological data: the partial correlation network. We describe how regularization techniques can be used to efficiently estimate a parsimonious and interpretable network structure in psychological data. We show how to perform these analyses in R and demonstrate the method in an empirical example on posttraumatic stress disorder data.
