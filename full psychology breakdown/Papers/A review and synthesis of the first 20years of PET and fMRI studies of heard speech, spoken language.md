**Authors:** Cathy J. Price
**Year:** 2012
**Citations:** 2,136
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2012.04.062

## Abstract

The anatomy of language has been investigated with PET or fMRI for more than 20 years. Here I attempt to provide an overview of the brain areas associated with heard speech, speech production and reading. The conclusions of many hundreds of studies were considered, grouped according to the type of processing, and reported in the order that they were published. Many findings have been replicated time and time again leading to some consistent and undisputable conclusions. These are summarised in an anatomical model that indicates the location of the language areas and the most consistent functions that have been assigned to them.
