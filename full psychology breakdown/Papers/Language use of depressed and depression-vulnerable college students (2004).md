# Language use of depressed and depression-vulnerable college students

**Authors:** Stephanie S. Rude, James W. Pennebaker
**Year:** 2004
**Citations:** 1,023
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/02699930441000030

## Abstract

Essays written by currently-depressed, formerly-depressed, and never-depressed college students were examined for differences in language that might shed light on the cognitive operations associated with depression and depression-vulnerability. A text analysis program computed the incidence of words in predesignated categories. Consistent with Beck's cognitive model and with Pyczsinski and Greenberg's self-focus model of depression, depressed participants used more negatively valenced words and used the word, "I" more than did never-depressed participants.
