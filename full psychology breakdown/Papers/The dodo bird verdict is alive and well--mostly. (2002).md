# The dodo bird verdict is alive and well--mostly.

**Authors:** Lester Luborsky, Elizabeth D. Krause
**Year:** 2002
**Citations:** 484
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/clipsy.9.1.2

## Abstract

We examined 17 meta-analyses of comparisons of active treatments with each other, in contrast to the more usual comparisons of active treatments with controls. These meta-analyses yielded a mean uncorrected absolute effect size for Cohen's d of .20, which is small and non-significant (an equivalent Pearson's r would be. 10). The smallness of this effect size confirms Rosenzweig's supposition in 1936 about the likely results of such comparisons.
