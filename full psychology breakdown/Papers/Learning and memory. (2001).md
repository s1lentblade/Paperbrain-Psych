## Abstract

After establishing the distinction between different learning processes (behavioral learning, skill acquisition and information acquisition processes), the specific learning phenomena belonging to each of these varieties are analyzed. Associative learning is described as a behavioral (pavlovian conditioning and instrumental learning) and cognitive (predictive learning and categorization) process. Also described are the properties of perceptual and motor learning, as those of the processes by which cognitive skills (e.g., rule learning) are acquired.
