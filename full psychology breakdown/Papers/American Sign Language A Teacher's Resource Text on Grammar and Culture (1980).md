# American Sign Language: A Teacher's Resource Text on Grammar and Culture

**Authors:** Charlotte Baker-Shenk, Dennis Cokely
**Year:** 1980
**Citations:** 498

## Abstract

Best known as the Green Books, the American Sign Language books provide teachers and students of American Sign Language (ASL) with the complete means for learning about the culture, community, and the native language of Deaf people. A group of 15 ASL teachers and linguists reviewed all five books to ensure that they were accurate and easy to comprehend. This volume of the American Sign Language series explains in depth the grammar and structure of ASL while also presenting a description of the Deaf community in the United States.
