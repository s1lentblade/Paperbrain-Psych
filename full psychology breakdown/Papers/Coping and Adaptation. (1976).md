## Abstract

For a long time it has been my contention that mental health is adequate maturity plus adjustment. For the adequately matune person, coping and adaptation are pretty much what living is all about; therefore, a book on this subject is more than welcome. This volume is a follow-up of the Conference on Coping and Adaptation held in Palo Alto, Calif., March 20-22, 1969. The conference was sponsored by Stanford University’s Department of Psychiatry and supported by NIMH. A group of topnotch people, leaders in their fields, have contributed to this multiauthor work on coping and adaptation, an excellent topic for interdisciplinary study.
