Tabes, Spinal Cyst.-i.A man of twenty-seven had had syphilis at the age of twenty-one.Later he developed attacks of continuous vomiting with hyperacidity, in consequence of which he lost weight.He had Argyll-Robertson pupils, diminished reflexes; some delay in sensation in the soles of the feet, and a small quantity of intensely acid fluid was always found in the stomach.Later he developed ataxia with pains in the arms, legs and back.The case is one of precocious tabes with gastric crises.2.
