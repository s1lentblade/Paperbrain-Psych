# Positive affect facilitates creative problem solving.

**Authors:** Alice M. Isen, Gary P. Nowicki
**Year:** 1987
**Citations:** 2,214
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037//0022-3514.52.6.1122

## Abstract

Four experiments indicated that positive affect, induced by means of seeing a few minutes of a comedy film or by means of receiving a small bag of candy, improved performance on two tasks that are generally regarded as requiring creative ingenuity: Duncker's (1945) candle task and M. T. Mednick, S. A. Mednick, and E. V. Mednick's (1964) Remote Associates Test. One condition in which negative affect was induced and two in which subjects engaged in physical exercise (intended to represent affectless arousal) failed to produce comparable improvements in creative performance.
