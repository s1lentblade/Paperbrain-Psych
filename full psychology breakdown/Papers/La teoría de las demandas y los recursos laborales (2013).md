# La teoría de las demandas y los recursos laborales

**Authors:** Arnold B. Bakker, Evangelia Demerouti
**Year:** 2013
**Citations:** 193
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5093/tr2013a16

## Abstract

The question of what causes job stress and what motivates people has received a lot of research attention during the past five decades. In this paper, we discuss Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) theory, which represents an extension of the Job Demands-Resources model (Bakker & Demerouti, 2007; JD-R theory explains how job demands and resources have unique and multiplicative effects on job stress and motivation, and proposes the existence of reciprocal relationships between these variables.
