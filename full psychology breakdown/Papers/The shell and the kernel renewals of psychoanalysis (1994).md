# The shell and the kernel : renewals of psychoanalysis

**Authors:** Nicolas Abraham, Nicholas Rand
**Year:** 1994
**Citations:** 640

## Abstract

Abraham and Torok advocate a form of psychoanalysis that insists on the particularity of any individual's life story, the specificity of texts and the singularity of historical situations. In what is both a critique and an extension of Freud, they develop interpretive strategies with implications for clinicians, literary theorists, feminists, philosophers and all others interested in the uses and limits of psychoanalysis. Central to their approach is a general theory of psychic concealment, a poetics of hiding.
