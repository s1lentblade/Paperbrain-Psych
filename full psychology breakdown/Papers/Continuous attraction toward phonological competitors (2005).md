**Authors:** Michael J. Spivey, Günther Knoblich
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 674
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0503903102

## Abstract

Certain models of spoken-language processing, like those for many other perceptual and cognitive processes, posit continuous uptake of sensory input and dynamic competition between simultaneously active representations. Here, we provide compelling evidence for this continuity assumption by using a continuous response, hand movements, to track the temporal dynamics of lexical activations during real-time spoken-word recognition in a visual context.
