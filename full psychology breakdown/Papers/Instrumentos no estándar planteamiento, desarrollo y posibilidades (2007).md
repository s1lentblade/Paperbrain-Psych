## Abstract

Non-standard instruments have, traditionally, received little attention despite being increasingly used during recent years. There are many situations in which eliciting the response is not the main interest but rather the construction of instruments, within the context of observational methodology, by inputting certain aspects of reality into a system that has been developed ad hoc. Deciding which the most suitable instrument is will depend on the observational design proposed in each case.
