# Introducing compassion-focused therapy

**Authors:** Paul Gilbert
**Year:** 2009
**Citations:** 1,379
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1192/apt.bp.107.005264

## Abstract

Summary Shame and self-criticism are transdiagnostic problems. People who experience them may struggle to feel relieved, reassured or safe. Research suggests that a specialised affect regulation system (or systems) underpins feelings of reassurance, safeness and well-being. It is believed to have evolved with attachment systems and, in particular, the ability to register and respond with calming and a sense of well-being to being cared for. In compassion-focused therapy it is hypothesised that this affect regulation system is poorly accessible in people with high shame and self-criticism, in whom the ‘threat’ affect regulation system dominates orientation to their inner and outer worlds.
