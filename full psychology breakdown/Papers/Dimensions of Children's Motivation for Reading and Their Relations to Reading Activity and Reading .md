ABSTRACTS Dimensions of children's motivation for reading and their relations to reading activity and reading achievement This study was designed to assess dimensions of reading motivation and examine how these dimensions related to students' reading activity and achievement. A heterogeneous urban sample of fifth‐ and sixth‐grade children completed the Motivation for Reading Questionnaire (MRQ; Wigfield &amp; Guthrie, 1997), a questionnaire designed to assess 11 possible dimensions of reading motivation, including self‐efficacy, several types of intrinsic and extrinsic reading motives, social aspects of reading, and the desire to avoid reading.
