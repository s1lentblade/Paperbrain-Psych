## Abstract

I will examine the organization and development of seventy transactions ending in murder. In all seventy cases, murder was the culmination of an interchange between an offender and victim, resembling what Goffman termed a “character contest,” a confrontation in which opponents sought to establish or maintain “face” at the other's expense by remaining steady in the face of adversity.
