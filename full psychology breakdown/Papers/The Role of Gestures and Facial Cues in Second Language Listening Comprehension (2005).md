# The Role of Gestures and Facial Cues in Second Language Listening Comprehension

**Authors:** Ayano Sueyoshi, Debra M. Hardison
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 360
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/j.0023-8333.2005.00320.x

## Abstract

This study investigated the contribution of gestures and facial cues to second-language learners’ listening comprehension of a videotaped lecture by a native speaker of English. A total of 42 low-intermediate and advanced learners of English as a second language were randomly assigned to 3 stimulus conditions: AV-gesture-face (audiovisual including gestures and face), AV-face (no gestures), and Audio-only. Results of a multiple-choice comprehension task revealed significantly better scores with visual cues for both proficiency levels.
