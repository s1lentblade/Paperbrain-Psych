# Female bisexuality from adolescence to adulthood: Results from a 10-year longitudinal study.

**Authors:** Lisa M. Diamond
**Year:** 2008
**Citations:** 559
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0012-1649.44.1.5

## Abstract

Debates persist over whether bisexuality is a temporary stage of denial or transition, a stable "3rd type" of sexual orientation, or a heightened capacity for sexual fluidity. The present study uses 5 waves of longitudinal data collected from 79 lesbian, bisexual, and "unlabeled" women to evaluate these models. Both the "3rd orientation" and "fluidity" models had support, but the "transitional stage" model did not. Over 10 years, 2/3 of women changed the identity labels they had claimed at the beginning of the study, and 1/3 changed labels 2 or more times.
