# The impact of overtime and long work hours on occupational injuries and illnesses: new evidence from the United States

**Authors:** Allard E. Dembe, Steven M. Banks
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 785
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1136/oem.2004.016667

## Abstract

Results suggest that job schedules with long working hours are not more risky merely because they are concentrated in inherently hazardous industries or occupations, or because people working long hours spend more total time "at risk" for a work injury. Strategies to prevent work injuries should consider changes in scheduling practices, job redesign, and health protection programmes for people working in jobs involving overtime and extended hours.
