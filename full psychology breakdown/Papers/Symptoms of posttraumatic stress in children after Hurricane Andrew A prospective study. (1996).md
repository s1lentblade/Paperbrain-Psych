# Symptoms of posttraumatic stress in children after Hurricane Andrew: A prospective study.

**Authors:** Annette M. La Greca, Mitchell J. Prinstein
**Year:** 1996
**Citations:** 712
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-006x.64.4.712

## Abstract

The authors examined symptoms of posttraumatic stress in 3rd-5th grade children during the school year after Hurricane Andrew. From a conceptual model of the effects of traumatic events, 442 children were evaluated 3, 7, and 10 months postdisaster with respect to (a) their exposure to traumatic events during and after the disaster, (b) their preexisting demographic characteristics, (c) the occurrence of major life stressors, (d) the availability of social support, and (e) the type of coping strategies used to cope with disaster-related distress.
