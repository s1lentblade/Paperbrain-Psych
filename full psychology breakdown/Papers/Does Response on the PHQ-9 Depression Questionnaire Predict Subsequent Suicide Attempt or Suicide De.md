**Authors:** Gregory E. Simon, Evette Ludman
**Year:** 2013
**Citations:** 553
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1176/appi.ps.201200587

## Abstract

OBJECTIVE As use of standard depression questionnaires in clinical practice increases, clinicians will frequently encounter patients reporting thoughts of death or suicide. This study examined whether responses to the Patient Health Questionnaire for depression (PHQ-9) predict subsequent suicide attempt or suicide death. METHODS Electronic records from a large integrated health system were used to link PHQ-9 responses from outpatient visits to subsequent suicide attempts and suicide deaths.
