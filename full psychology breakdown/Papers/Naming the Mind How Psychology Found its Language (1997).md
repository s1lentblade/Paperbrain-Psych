## Abstract

In 1899 H. S. Jennings published a paper entitled psychology of the protozoan. In 1904 he published another called behavior of the Paramecium. The second edition of Ernst Meumann's book on the economy and technique of memory was published in 1908; its third edition, translated into English and published in 1913, was called The Psychology of Learning. These events serve as markers for the historical introduction into scientific practice of the two categories that would become fundamental to American psychology by the middle of this century: and learning.
