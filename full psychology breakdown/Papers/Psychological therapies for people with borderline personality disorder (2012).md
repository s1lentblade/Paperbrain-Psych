# Psychological therapies for people with borderline personality disorder

**Authors:** Jutta Stoffers‐Winterling, Klaus Lieb
**Year:** 2012
**Citations:** 905
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/14651858.cd005652.pub2

## Abstract

Our assessments showed beneficial effects on all primary outcomes in favour of BPD-tailored psychotherapy compared with TAU. However, only the outcome of BPD severity reached the MIREDIF-defined cut-off for a clinically meaningful improvement. Subgroup analyses found no evidence of a difference in effect estimates between the different types of therapies (compared to TAU) . The pooled analysis of psychotherapy versus waiting list or no treatment found significant improvement on BPD severity, psychosocial functioning and depression at end of treatment, but these findings were based on low-quality evidence, and the true magnitude of these effects is uncertain.
