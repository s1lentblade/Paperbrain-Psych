# Factors predicting the subjective well-being of nations.

**Authors:** Ed Diener, Carol Diener
**Year:** 1995
**Citations:** 1,394
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037//0022-3514.69.5.851

## Abstract

Subjective well-being (SWB) in 55 nations, reported in probability surveys and a large college student sample, was correlated with social, economic, and cultural characteristics of the nations. The SWB surveys, representing nations that include three fourths of the earth's population, showed strong convergence. Separate measures of the predictor variables also converged and formed scales with high reliability, with the exception of the comparison variables. High income, individualism, human rights, and societal equality correlated strongly with each other, and with SWB across surveys.
