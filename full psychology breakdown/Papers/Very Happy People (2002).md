# Very Happy People

**Authors:** Ed Diener, Martin E. P. Seligman
**Year:** 2002
**Citations:** 2,338
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/1467-9280.00415

## Abstract

A sample of 222 undergraduates was screened for high happiness using multiple confirming assessment filters. We compared the upper 10% of consistently very happy people with average and very unhappy people. The very happy people were highly social, and had stronger romantic and other social relationships than less happy groups. They were more extraverted, more agreeable, and less neurotic, and scored lower on several psychopathology scales of the Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory. Compared with the less happy groups, the happiest respondents did not exercise significantly more, participate in religious activities significantly more, or experience more objectively defined good event
