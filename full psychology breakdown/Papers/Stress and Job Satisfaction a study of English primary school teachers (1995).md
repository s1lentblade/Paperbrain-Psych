# Stress and Job Satisfaction: a study of English primary school teachers

**Authors:** Roland Chaplain
**Year:** 1995
**Citations:** 145
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/0144341950150409

## Abstract

Abstract Using a self‐report questionnaire, a picture of the sources of stress and job satisfaction amongst a sample of 267 teachers, drawn from primary schools in the North and Eastern regions of England, is established. Teachers scored the frequency and intensity of 18 items on a stress scale. A principal components analysis was carried out and three factors were identified: professional concerns, pupil behaviour and attitude and professional tasks. The strongest correlations were found between professional concerns and occupational stress. Biographical factors were examined and significant differences were found between men and women, and teachers of different ages and length of teaching
