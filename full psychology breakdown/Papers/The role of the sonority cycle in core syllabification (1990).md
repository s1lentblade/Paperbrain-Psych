## Abstract

One of the major concerns of laboratory phonology is that of determining the nature of the transition between discrete phonological structure (conventionally, "phonology") and its expression in terms of nondiscrete physical or psychoacoustic parameters (conventionally, "phonetics"). A considerable amount of research has been devoted to determining where this transition lies, and to what extent the rule types and representational systems needed to characterize the two levels may differ (see Keating 1985 for an overview).
