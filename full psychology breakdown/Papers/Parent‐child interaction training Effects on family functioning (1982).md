# Parent‐child interaction training: Effects on family functioning

**Authors:** Sheila M. Eyberg, Elizabeth A. Robinson
**Year:** 1982
**Citations:** 317
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/15374418209533076

## Abstract

Parent‐child interaction training was designed for the treatment of disturbed preschool children and their families. This paper describes the treatment and its effects on seven referred children, their untreated siblings, and the psychological functioning of their parents using multiple process and outcome measures. High rates of attendance, home practice, cooperation, and satisfaction with treatment; significant pre‐ to post‐treatment changes in child and sibling behavior; and improvement in parental adjustment were found.
