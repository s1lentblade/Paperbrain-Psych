## Abstract

This article uses the concept of “political pharmacology” to show that drugs are complex and mutable entities, the constitution of which is as much a political (power) issue as it is a technical one. The article analyzes the negotiations and struggles that have been involved in the constitution of methadone and heroin as maintenance drugs in Denmark. Danish drug policy on medical-maintenance treatment was for many years dominated by a medico-administrative technocracy. But from the mid-1990s, this technocracy, and the way it defined maintenance treatment and maintenance drugs, was challenged by drug users and other actors.
