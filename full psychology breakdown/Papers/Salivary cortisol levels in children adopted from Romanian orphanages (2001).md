# Salivary cortisol levels in children adopted from Romanian orphanages

**Authors:** Megan R. Gunnar, Michelle R. Schuder
**Year:** 2001
**Citations:** 517
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1017/s095457940100311x

## Abstract

Six and a half years after adoption. 6- to 12-year-old children reared in Romanian orphanages for more than 8 months in their first years of life (RO. n = 18) had higher cortisol levels over the daytime hours than did early adopted (EA, < or = 4 months of age, n = 15) and Canadian born (CB, n = 27) children. The effect was marked, with 22% of the RO children exhibiting cortisol levels averaged over the day that exceeded the mean plus 2 SD of the EA and CB levels. Furthermore, the longer beyond 8 months that the RO children remained institutionalized the higher their cortisol levels.
