## Abstract

This paper initiated development of a theory of recreation specialization from a social worlds perspective and provided empirical testing for some of the stated propositions. Recreation specialization was re-conceptualized as 1) a process by which recreation social worlds and subworlds segment and intersect into new recreation subworlds and 2) the subsequent ordered arrangement of these subworlds and their members along a continuum. At one end of the continuum is the least specialized subworld and its members and at the other end is the most specialized subworld and its members.
