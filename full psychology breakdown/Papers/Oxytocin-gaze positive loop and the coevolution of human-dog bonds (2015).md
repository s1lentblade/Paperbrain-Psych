# Oxytocin-gaze positive loop and the coevolution of human-dog bonds

**Authors:** Miho Nagasawa, Takefumi Kikusui
**Year:** 2015
**Citations:** 708
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1261022

## Abstract

Human-like modes of communication, including mutual gaze, in dogs may have been acquired during domestication with humans. We show that gazing behavior from dogs, but not wolves, increased urinary oxytocin concentrations in owners, which consequently facilitated owners' affiliation and increased oxytocin concentration in dogs. Further, nasally administered oxytocin increased gazing behavior in dogs, which in turn increased urinary oxytocin concentrations in owners.
