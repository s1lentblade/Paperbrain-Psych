# It's All in the Timing: Interpersonal Synchrony Increases Affiliation

**Authors:** Michael J. Hove, Jane L. Risen
**Year:** 2009
**Citations:** 1,313
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1521/soco.2009.27.6.949

## Abstract

The tendency to mimic and synchronize with others is well established. Although mimicry has been shown to lead to affiliation between co-actors, the effect of interpersonal synchrony on affiliation remains an open question. The authors investigated the relationship by having participants match finger movements with a visual moving metronome. In Experiment 1, affiliation ratings were examined based on the extent to which participants tapped in synchrony with the experimenter. In Experiment 2, synchrony was manipulated.
