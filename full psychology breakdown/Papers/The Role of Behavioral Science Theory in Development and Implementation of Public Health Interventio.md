# The Role of Behavioral Science Theory in Development and Implementation of Public Health Interventions

**Authors:** Karen Glanz, Donald B. Bishop
**Year:** 2010
**Citations:** 2,347
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev.publhealth.012809.103604

## Abstract

Increasing evidence suggests that public health and health-promotion interventions that are based on social and behavioral science theories are more effective than those lacking a theoretical base. This article provides an overview of the state of the science of theory use for designing and conducting health-promotion interventions. Influential contemporary perspectives stress the multiple determinants and multiple levels of determinants of health and health behavior. We describe key types of theory and selected often-used theories and their key concepts, including the health belief model, the transtheoretical model, social cognitive theory, and the ecological model.
