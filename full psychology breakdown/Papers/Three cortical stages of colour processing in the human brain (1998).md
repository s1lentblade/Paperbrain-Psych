# Three cortical stages of colour processing in the human brain

**Authors:** Semir Zeki
**Year:** 1998
**Citations:** 286
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/brain/121.9.1669

## Abstract

We used the technique of functional magnetic resonance imaging to chart the colour pathways in the human brain beyond V4. We asked subjects to view objects that were dressed in natural and unnatural colours as well as their achromatic counterparts and compared the activity produced in the brain by each condition. The results showed that both naturally and unnaturally coloured objects activate a pathway extending from V1 to V4, though not overlapping totally the activity produced by viewing abstract coloured Mondrian scenes.
