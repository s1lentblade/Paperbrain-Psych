# Cross-cultural correlates of life satisfaction and self-esteem.

**Authors:** Ed Diener, Ed Diener
**Year:** 1995
**Citations:** 1,845
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037//0022-3514.68.4.653

## Abstract

College students in 31 nations (N = 13,118) completed measures of self-esteem, life satisfaction, and satisfaction with specific domains (friends, family, and finances). The authors assessed whether cross-cultural variations in the strength of associations were related to societal dimensions including income and individualism. At the national level, individualism correlated -.24 (ns) with heterogeneity and .71 (p < .001) with wealth. At the individual level, self-esteem and life satisfaction were correlated .47 for the entire sample. This relation, however, was moderated by the individualism of the society.
