## Abstract

<h3>Background:</h3> This research study evaluates the effectiveness of a multifaceted intervention program to improve the management of depression in primary care. <h3>Methods:</h3> One hundred fifty-three primary care patients with current depression were entered into a randomized controlled trial. Intervention patients received a structured depression treatment program in the primary care setting that included both behavioral treatment to increase use of adaptive coping strategies and counseling to improve medication adherence.
