**Year:** 1939

## Abstract

This monograph brings together and expands Dr. Bender's previous publications on visual motor gestalt function. The author assumes a familiarity with the classic teachings of gestalt psychology, which are not reviewed in the book. She defines the gestalt function as "that function of the integrated organism whereby it responds to a given constellation of stimuli as a whole; the response itself being a constellation, or pattern, or gestalt." The material offered is based on studies of psychiatric patients, of normal adults and children by use of a series of patterns first devised by Max Wertheimer.
