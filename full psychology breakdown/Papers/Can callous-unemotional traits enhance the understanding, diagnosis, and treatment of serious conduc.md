**Authors:** Paul J. Frick, Rachel E. Kahn
**Year:** 2013
**Citations:** 1,207
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/a0033076

## Abstract

This article provides a comprehensive review of the research on the use of callous and unemotional (CU) traits for designating an important subgroup of children and adolescents with severe conduct problems. It focuses on the etiological significance of recognizing this subgroup of youths with severe conduct problems, its implications for diagnostic classification, and the treatment implications of this research. The review highlights limitations in existing research and provides directions for future research.
