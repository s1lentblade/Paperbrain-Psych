# Psychosocial risk factors for future adolescent suicide attempts.

**Authors:** Peter M. Lewinsohn, John R. Seeley
**Year:** 1994
**Citations:** 560
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037//0022-006x.62.2.297

## Abstract

An array of psychosocial risk factors for making a suicide attempt were examined in a representative sample of 1,508 older (14- to 18-year-old) high school students, 26 of whom made a suicide attempt during the year following entry into the study. Strongest predictors of future suicide attempt were history of past attempt, current suicidal ideation and depression, recent attempt by a friend, low self-esteem, and having been born to a teenage mother. The results suggest that adolescents who are depressed and those who attempt suicide share many psychosocial risk factors.
