# Parental language input patterns and children's bilingual use

**Authors:** Annick De Houwer
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 719
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1017/s0142716407070221

## Abstract

This article reports on a study that addresses the following question: why do some children exposed to two languages from early on fail to speak those two languages? Questionnaire data were collected in 1,899 families in which at least one of the parents spoke a language other than the majority language. Each questionnaire asked about the home language use of a family consisting of at least one parent and one child between the ages of 6 and 10 years old. The results show that the children in these families all spoke the majority language, but that minority language use was not universal.
