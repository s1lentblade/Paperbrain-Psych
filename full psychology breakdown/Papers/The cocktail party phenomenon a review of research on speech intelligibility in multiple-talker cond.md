# The cocktail party phenomenon : a review of research on speech intelligibility in multiple-talker conditions

**Authors:** Adelbert W. Bronkhorst
**Year:** 2000
**Citations:** 819

## Abstract

It is in most cases relatively easy to understand one talker even if other persons arc talking at the same time. Over the last decades, this so-called cocktail party phenomenon has been the subject of a considerable number of studies. In the present paper, an overview is given of part of this research, specifically those studies dealing with the intelligibility of speech presented against a background of competing speech. In the first section of the review, the properties of speech are considered that are relevant when it acts as an interfering sound, the long-term average frequency spectrum, and the modulation spectrum.
