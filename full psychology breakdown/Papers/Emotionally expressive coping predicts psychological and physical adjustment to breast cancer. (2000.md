# Emotionally expressive coping predicts psychological and physical adjustment to breast cancer.

**Authors:** Annette L. Stanton, Robert K. Twillman
**Year:** 2000
**Citations:** 772
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-006x.68.5.875

## Abstract

This study tested the hypothesis that coping through emotional approach, which involves actively processing and expressing emotions, enhances adjustment and health status for breast cancer patients. Patients (n = 92) completed measures within 20 weeks following medical treatment and 3 months later.
