## Abstract

The motive to avoid success is conceptualized within the framework of an expectancy‐value theory of motivation. It is identified as an internal psychological representative of the dominant societal stereotype which views competence, independence, competition, and intellectual achievement as qualities basically inconsistent with femininity even though positively related to masculinity and mental health. The expectancy that success in achievement‐related situations will be followed by negative consequences arouses fear of success in otherwise achievement‐motivated women which then inhibits their performance and levels of aspiration.
