# Leader appeal, leader performance, and the motive profiles of leaders and followers: A study of American presidents and elections.

**Authors:** David G. Winter
**Year:** 1987
**Citations:** 384
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.52.1.196

## Abstract

Three leader trait and leader-follower interaction models of leader appeal and leader performance are evaluated with data about he motive profiles of American presidents and American society, in both cases measured at a distance. Presidential ppeal, defined in terms of electoral success, is significantly correlated with the congruence or match between the president&amp;apos;s motive profile and that of his contemporary society. In contrast, presidential greatness, as rated by historians, as well as several important outcomes involving war and peace are associated with certain of the president&amp;apos;s motives by themselves, but not with president-society congruence. What is a great leader?
