## Abstract

CAMPBELL, JOHN D. Illness Is a Point of View: The Development of Children's Concepts of Illness. CHILD DEVELOPMENT, 1975, 46, 92-100. To study development of concepts of illness, 264 children (6 to 12-11) and their mothers were interviewed. Comparison of group profiles showed that the typical definition given by children was similar to that of mothers: themes used by many mothers were often used by children; those rare in mothers' definitions infrequently occurred in children's. Yet comparison of mother-child pairs revealed that the illness concept of any specific child was not likely to resemble that 'of his parent.
