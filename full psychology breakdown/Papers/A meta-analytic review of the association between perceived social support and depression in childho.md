# A meta-analytic review of the association between perceived social support and depression in childhood and adolescence.

**Authors:** Sandra Yu Rueger, Samantha Coyle
**Year:** 2016
**Citations:** 875
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/bul0000058

## Abstract

This meta-analysis evaluated the relation between social support and depression in youth and compared the cumulative evidence for 2 theories that have been proposed to explain this association: the general benefits (GB; also known as main effects) and stress-buffering (SB) models. The study included 341 articles (19% unpublished) gathered through a search in PsycINFO, PsycARTICLES, ERIC, and ProQuest, and a hand search of 11 relevant journals. Using a random effects model, the overall effect size based on k = 341 studies and N = 273,149 participants was r = .26 (95% CI [.24, .28]), with robust support for the GB model and support for the SB model among medically ill youth.
