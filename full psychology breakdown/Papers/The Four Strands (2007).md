# The Four Strands

**Authors:** Paul Nation
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 481
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.2167/illt039.0

## Abstract

The activities in a language course can be classified into the four strands of meaning-focused input, meaning-focused output, language-focused learning and fluency development. In a well designed course there should be an even balance of these strands with roughly equal amounts of time given to each strand. The research evidence for the strands draws on the input hypothesis and learning from extensive reading, the output hypothesis, research on form-focused instruction, and the development of speaking and reading fluency.
