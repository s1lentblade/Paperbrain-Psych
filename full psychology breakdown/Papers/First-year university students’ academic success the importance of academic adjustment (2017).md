# First-year university students’ academic success: the importance of academic adjustment

**Authors:** Els van Rooij, Wim van de Grift
**Year:** 2017
**Citations:** 274
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s10212-017-0347-8

## Abstract

Considering the pivotal role of academic adjustment for student success, it is important to gain insight into how several motivational and behavioural factors affect academic adjustment and the extent to which academic adjustment influences student success. This empirical study investigated how intrinsic motivation, academic self-efficacy, self-regulated study behaviour and satisfaction with the chosen degree programme influenced academic adjustment in university and how these variables and adjustment affected three important indicators of student success: grade point average (GPA), attained number of credits (ECTS) and intention to persist.
