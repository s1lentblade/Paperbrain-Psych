# Neuroimaging and frontal-subcortical circuitry in obsessive-compulsive disorder

**Authors:** S Saxena, L.R. Baxter
**Year:** 1998
**Citations:** 834
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1192/s0007125000297870

## Abstract

OCD symptoms are mediated by hyperactivity in orbitofrontal-subcortical circuits, perhaps due to an imbalance of tone between direct and indirect striato-pallidal pathways. We present a model which describes how frontal-subcortical brain circuitry may mediate OCD symptomatology, and suggest a hypothesis for how successful treatments may ameliorate symptoms, via their effects on circuit activity.
