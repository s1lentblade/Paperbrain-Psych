# Psychometric Assessment of an Instrument Designed to Measure Work Life Balance

**Authors:** Jeremy Hayman
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 261

## Abstract

Work family conflict and work life balance issues have received a great deal of attention from researchers and contemporary employers. Nevertheless, there is a lack of reported scales for assessing the construct of work life balance. This study evaluated a 15 item scale for assessing the construct of work life balance adapted from an instrument reported by Fisher-McAuley, Stanton, Jolton and Gavin (2001) with data obtained from 61 human resource administrators of a large university in Western Australia.
