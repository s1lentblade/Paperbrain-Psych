# The theoretical status of latent variables.

**Authors:** Denny Borsboom, Jaap van Heerden
**Year:** 2003
**Citations:** 1,583
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295x.110.2.203

## Abstract

This article examines the theoretical status of latent variables as used in modern test theory models. First, it is argued that a consistent interpretation of such models requires a realist ontology for latent variables. Second, the relation between latent variables and their indicators is discussed. It is maintained that this relation can be interpreted as a causal one but that in measurement models for interindividual differences the relation does not apply to the level of the individual person.
