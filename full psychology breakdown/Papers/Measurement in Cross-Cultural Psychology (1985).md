# Measurement in Cross-Cultural Psychology

**Authors:** C. Harry Hui, Harry C. Triandis
**Year:** 1985
**Citations:** 1,095
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/0022002185016002001

## Abstract

Notions of equivalence in cross-cultural measurement were related to the abstraction-concreteness and the universality-cultural difference continua. Various methods proposed for attaining satisfactory measurement were reviewed and compared within this framework. Each strategy has its own merits and shortcomings. Moreover, the level of cross-cultural equivalence presupposed, the type of equivalence demonstrated and/or improved, and the equivalence assumptions doubted or explicitly rejected are different for different strategies.
