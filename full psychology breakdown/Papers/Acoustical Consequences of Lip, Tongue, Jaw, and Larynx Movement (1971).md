## Abstract

An articulatory model is presented. It defines a procedure for deriving a set of formant frequencies from information on the state of the lip muscles, the position of the jaw, the shape and position of the tongue body, and larynx height. The acoustic and auditory consequences of varying these parameters individually are reported. The introduction of the jaw as a separate parameter—a feature not used in previous articulatory models—makes it possible to explain why “openness” occurs as a universal phonetic feature of vowel production.
