# Reasons for Studying: Motivational Orientations and Study Strategies

**Authors:** Susan Bobbitt Nolen
**Year:** 1988
**Citations:** 681
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1207/s1532690xci0504_2

## Abstract

Abstract This study explored the relationship among (a) individual differences in three motivational or goal orientations and (b) valuing and use of study strategies by eighth graders reading expository passages. Task orientation (the goal of learning or understanding for its own sake) was positively correlated with both perceived value and use of strategies requiring deep processing of information. To a lesser degree, task orientation correlated with valuing and use of strategies requiring only surface-level processing.
