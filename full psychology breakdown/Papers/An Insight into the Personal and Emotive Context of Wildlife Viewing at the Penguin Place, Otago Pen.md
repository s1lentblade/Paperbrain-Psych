**Authors:** Heike Schänzel, Alison McIntosh
**Year:** 2000
**Citations:** 178
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/09669580008667348

## Abstract

Abstract The personal and emotive context of visitors' experiences has been neglected in much sustainable tourist attraction management. This paper applies ASEB demi-grid analysis as a consumer-orientated management tool to facilitate an understanding of the beneficial experiences gained by international visitors to a penguin-watching tourism attraction on the Otago Peninsula, New Zealand. Forty in-depth interviews with visitors showed that the main beneficial experiences gained included reported enhanced environmental awareness (cognition) and 'mood' benefits (affection).
