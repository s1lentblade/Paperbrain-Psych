## Abstract

The Pearson product–moment correlation coefficient (<i>r<sub>p</sub></i>) and the Spearman rank correlation coefficient (<i>r<sub>s</sub></i>) are widely used in psychological research. We compare <i>r<sub>p</sub></i> and <i>r<sub>s</sub></i> on 3 criteria: variability, bias with respect to the population value, and robustness to an outlier.
