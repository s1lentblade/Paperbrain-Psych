# Marital interaction: Physiological linkage and affective exchange.

**Authors:** Robert W. Levenson, John M. Gottman
**Year:** 1983
**Citations:** 829
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.45.3.587

## Abstract

Thirty married couples were studied during naturalistic interactions to determine the extent to which variation in marital satisfaction could be accounted for by physiological and affective patterns between and within spouses. The authors hypothesized that (a) compared to nondistressed couples' interactions, distressed couples' interactions would show greater physiological interrelatedness or "linkage," more negative affect, and more reciprocity of negative affect and (b) these differences would be more pronounced when the interaction was high in conflict (discussing a marital problem) as opposed to low in conflict (discussing the events of the day).
