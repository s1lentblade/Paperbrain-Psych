# On Lying and Being Lied To: A Linguistic Analysis of Deception in Computer-Mediated Communication

**Authors:** Jeffrey T. Hancock, Michael Woodworth
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 520
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/01638530701739181

## Abstract

This study investigated changes in both the liar's and the conversational partner's linguistic style across truthful and deceptive dyadic communication in a synchronous text-based setting. An analysis of 242 transcripts revealed that liars produced more words, more sense-based words (e.g., seeing, touching), and used fewer self-oriented but more other-oriented pronouns when lying than when telling the truth. In addition, motivated liars avoided causal terms when lying, whereas unmotivated liars tended to increase their use of negations.
