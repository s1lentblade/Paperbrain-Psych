# Gender, Sex Role Orientation, and Attitudes toward Animals

**Authors:** Harold A. Herzog, Robert B. Pittman
**Year:** 1991
**Citations:** 398

## Abstract

To examine the relationship among gender, sex role orientation, and attitudes toward the treatment of animals, 144 male and 222 female college students were administered the Bem Sex Role Inventory, a Likert-scale questionnaire designed to assess attitudes toward animal welfare issues, and a measure of perceived comfort touching animals of a variety of species. There were significant gender differences on all of the animal-related measures with the exception of self-reported comfort touching positively perceived animals.
