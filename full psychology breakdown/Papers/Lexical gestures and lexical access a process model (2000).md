## Abstract

Observers of human behavior have long been fascinated by the gestures that accompany speech, and by the contributions to communication they purportedly make.1 Yet, despite this long-standing fascination, remarkably little about such gestures is well understood. The list of things we don&amp;apos;t understand is long, but some of the most important open questions concern their form and function: why do different gestures take the particular form they do, and what it is that these ubiquitous behaviors accomplish?
