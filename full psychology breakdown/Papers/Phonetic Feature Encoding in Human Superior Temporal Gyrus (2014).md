# Phonetic Feature Encoding in Human Superior Temporal Gyrus

**Authors:** Nima Mesgarani, Edward F. Chang
**Year:** 2014
**Citations:** 1,048
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1245994

## Abstract

During speech perception, linguistic elements such as consonants and vowels are extracted from a complex acoustic speech signal. The superior temporal gyrus (STG) participates in high-order auditory processing of speech, but how it encodes phonetic information is poorly understood. We used high-density direct cortical surface recordings in humans while they listened to natural, continuous speech to reveal the STG representation of the entire English phonetic inventory. At single electrodes, we found response selectivity to distinct phonetic features. Encoding of acoustic properties was mediated by a distributed population response.
