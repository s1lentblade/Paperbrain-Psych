# A Meta-Analytic Review of Eating Disorder Prevention Programs: Encouraging Findings

**Authors:** Eric Stice, C. Nathan Marti
**Year:** 2006
**Citations:** 545
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev.clinpsy.3.022806.091447

## Abstract

This meta-analytic review found that 51% of eating disorder prevention programs reduced eating disorder risk factors and 29% reduced current or future eating pathology. Larger effects occurred for programs that were selected (versus universal), interactive (versus didactic), multisession (versus single session), solely offered to females (versus both sexes), offered to participants over 15 years of age (versus younger ones), and delivered by professional interventionists (versus endogenous providers).
