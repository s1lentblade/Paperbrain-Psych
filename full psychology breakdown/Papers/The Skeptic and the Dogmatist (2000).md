## Abstract

this paper is devoted to articulating and defending a dogmatist account of perceptual justification which enables us to reject the skeptic&amp;apos;s arguments that we have no justified perceptual beliefs. I will present the positive case for my account in III, and I will defend it against an important objection in IV. My primary concern will be questions about perceptual justification rather than questions about perceptual knowledge. This is because the connections between justification and knowledge are complicated.
