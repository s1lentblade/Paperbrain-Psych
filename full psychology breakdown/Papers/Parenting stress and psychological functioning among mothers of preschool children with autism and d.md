# Parenting stress and psychological functioning among mothers of preschool children with autism and developmental delay

**Authors:** Annette Estes, Robert D. Abbott
**Year:** 2009
**Citations:** 842
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/1362361309105658

## Abstract

Parents of children with developmental disabilities, particularly autism spectrum disorders (ASDs), are at risk for high levels of distress. The factors contributing to this are unclear. This study investigated how child characteristics influence maternal parenting stress and psychological distress. Participants consisted of mothers and developmental-age matched preschool-aged children with ASD (N = 51) and developmental delay without autism (DD) ( N = 22). Evidence for higher levels of parenting stress and psychological distress was found in mothers in the ASD group compared to the DD group.
