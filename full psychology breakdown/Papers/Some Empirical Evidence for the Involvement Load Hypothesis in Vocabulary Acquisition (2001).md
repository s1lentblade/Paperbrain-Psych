# Some Empirical Evidence for the Involvement Load Hypothesis in Vocabulary Acquisition

**Authors:** Jan H. Hulstijn, Batia Laufer
**Year:** 2001
**Citations:** 816
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/0023-8333.00164

## Abstract

EFL learners in two countries participated in two parallel experiments testing whether retention of vocabulary acquired incidentally is contingent on amount of task‐induced involvement. Short‐ and long‐term retention of ten unfamiliar words was investigated in three learning tasks (reading comprehension, comprehension plus filling in target words, and composition‐writing with target words) with varying “involvement loads”—various combinations of need, search, and evaluation. Time‐on‐task, regarded as inherent to a task, differed among all three tasks.
