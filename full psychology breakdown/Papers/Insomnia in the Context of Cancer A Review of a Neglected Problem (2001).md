# Insomnia in the Context of Cancer: A Review of a Neglected Problem

**Authors:** Josée Savard, Charles M. Morin
**Year:** 2001
**Citations:** 862
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1200/jco.2001.19.3.895

## Abstract

This article reviews the evidence on the diagnosis, epidemiology, etiology, and treatment of insomnia in the context of cancer and proposes several areas for future research. Clinical and diagnostic features of insomnia are described and prevalence estimates of insomnia complaints in cancer patients are summarized. Then, potential etiologic factors (ie, predisposing, precipitating, and perpetuating factors) and consequences of insomnia (ie, psychologic, behavioral, and health impact) in the context of cancer are discussed.
