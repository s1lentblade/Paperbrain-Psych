**Authors:** Nancy Eisenberg, Randy Lennon
**Year:** 1983
**Citations:** 1,260
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.94.1.100

## Abstract

In this article, the literature on sex differences in empathy (denned as vicarious affective responding to the emotional state of another) and related capacities (affective role taking and decoding of nonverbal cues) was reviewed. The literature was organized and discussed according to method used to assess empathy and affective role taking. Where appropriate, meta-analyses were also computed. In general, sex differences in empathy were a function of the methods used to assess empathy.
