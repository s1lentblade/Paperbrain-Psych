# The Relations of Effortful Control and Impulsivity to Children's Resiliency and Adjustment

**Authors:** Nancy Eisenberg, Marilyn S. Thompson
**Year:** 2004
**Citations:** 512
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-8624.2004.00652.x

## Abstract

The unique relations of effortful control and impulsivity to resiliency and adjustment were examined when children were 4.5 to 8 years old, and 2 years later. Parents and teachers reported on all constructs and children's attentional persistence was observed. In concurrent structural equation models, effortful control and impulsivity uniquely and directly predicted resiliency and externalizing problems and indirectly predicted internalizing problems (through resiliency). Teacher-reported anger moderated the relations of effortful control and impulsivity to externalizing problems. In the longitudinal model, all relations held at T2 except for the path from impulsivity to externalizing problem
