## Abstract

Ratings of mental symptoms, behaviour in the ward, and attitudes to discharge were made on random samples of male schizophrenic patients, aged under sixty, from two London mental hospitals. Those resident less than two years were omitted. There was a concentration of unfavourable attitudes to discharge in the longer‐stay groups which remained significant when age, and mental symptom category, were allowed for. There was no significant association between mental symptom category and length of stay. There was no significant association between ward behaviour and length of stay except for one single item.
