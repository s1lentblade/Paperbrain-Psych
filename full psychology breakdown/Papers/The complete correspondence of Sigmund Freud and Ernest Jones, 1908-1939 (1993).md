**Authors:** Sigmund Freud, R. Andrew Paskauskas
**Year:** 1993

## Abstract

Soon after their first meeting in 1908, Freud's future biographer, Ernest Jones, initiated a correspondence with the founder of psychoanalysis that would continue until Freud's death in London in 1939. Jones, a Welsh-born neurologist, would become a principal player in the development of psychoanalysis in England and the United States. This volume makes available, from British and American archives, nearly 700 previously unpublished letters, postcards and telegrams, the vast majority of the three-decade correspondence between Freud and his admiring younger colleague.
