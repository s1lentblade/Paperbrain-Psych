# Big brains, enhanced cognition, and response of birds to novel environments

**Authors:** Daniel Sol, Louis Lefebvre
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 913
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0408145102

## Abstract

The widely held hypothesis that enlarged brains have evolved as an adaptation to cope with novel or altered environmental conditions lacks firm empirical support. Here, we test this hypothesis for a major animal group (birds) by examining whether large-brained species show higher survival than small-brained species when introduced to nonnative locations. Using a global database documenting the outcome of >600 introduction events, we confirm that avian species with larger brains, relative to their body mass, tend to be more successful at establishing themselves in novel environments.
