**Authors:** Frances A. Champagne, Michael J. Meaney
**Year:** 2001
**Citations:** 708
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.221224598

## Abstract

Naturally occurring variations in maternal licking/grooming influence neural development and are transmitted from mother to female offspring. We found that the induction of maternal behavior in virgin females through constant exposure to pups (pup sensitization) was significantly shorter in the offspring of High compared with Low licking/grooming mothers, suggesting differences in maternal responsivity.
