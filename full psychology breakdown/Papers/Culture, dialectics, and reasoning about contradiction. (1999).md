# Culture, dialectics, and reasoning about contradiction.

**Authors:** Kaiping Peng, Richard E. Nisbett
**Year:** 1999
**Citations:** 1,671
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066x.54.9.741

## Abstract

Chinese ways of dealing with seeming contradictions result in a dialectical or compromise approach---retaining basic elements of opposing perspectives by seeking a &amp;quot;middle way.&amp;quot; European-American ways, on the other hand, deriving from a lay version of Aristotelian logic, result in a differentiation model that polarizes contradictory perspectives in an effort to determine which fact or position is correct. Empirical studies showed that dialectical thinking is a form of folk wisdom in Chinese culture: Chinese preferred dialectical proverbs containing seeming contradictions more than did Americans.
