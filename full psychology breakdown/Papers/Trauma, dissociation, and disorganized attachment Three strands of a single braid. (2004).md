## Abstract

The aim of this paper is to familiarize the reader with research findings and theoretical perspectives that suggest that disorganized attachment plays a central role in trauma-related disorders. It is proposed that the propensity to react to traumatic events with dissociation is related to disorganization of early attachment and its developmental sequelae. This conceptual framework has deep implications for the psychotherapeutic treatment of dissociative disorders, a topic that will be briefly addressed in the final section of this paper.
