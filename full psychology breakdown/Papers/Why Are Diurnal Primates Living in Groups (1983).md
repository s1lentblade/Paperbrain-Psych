# Why Are Diurnal Primates Living in Groups?

**Authors:** Carel P. van Schaik
**Year:** 1983
**Citations:** 1,018
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1163/156853983x00147

## Abstract

There are two main competing theories on the evolution of group living in diurnal nonhuman primates. The first theory claims that predation avoidance favours group living, whereas there are only disadvantages to feeding in a group and feeding competition increases with group size. The second theory claims that there is a feeding advantage to group living deriving from communal defence of high-quality food patches and that predation is not important. These theories have not yet been rigorously tested.
