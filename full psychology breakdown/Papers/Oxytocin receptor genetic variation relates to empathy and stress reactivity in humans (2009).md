# Oxytocin receptor genetic variation relates to empathy and stress reactivity in humans

**Authors:** Sarina M. Rodrigues, Dacher Keltner
**Year:** 2009
**Citations:** 818
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0909579106

## Abstract

Oxytocin, a peptide that functions as both a hormone and neurotransmitter, has broad influences on social and emotional processing throughout the body and the brain. In this study, we tested how a polymorphism (rs53576) of the oxytocin receptor relates to two key social processes related to oxytocin: empathy and stress reactivity. Compared with individuals homozygous for the G allele of rs53576 (GG), individuals with one or two copies of the A allele (AG/AA) exhibited lower behavioral and dispositional empathy, as measured by the "Reading the Mind in the Eyes" Test and an other-oriented empathy scale.
