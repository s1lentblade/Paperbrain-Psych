# Issues in the Classification of Multimodal Communication Signals

**Authors:** Sarah Partan, Peter Marler
**Year:** 2005
**Citations:** 587
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1086/431246

## Abstract

Communication involves complex behavior in multiple sensory channels, or "modalities." We provide an overview of multimodal communication and its costs and benefits, place examples of signals and displays from an array of taxa, sensory systems, and functions into our signal classification system, and consider issues surrounding the categorization of multimodal signals. The broadest level of classification is between signals with redundant and nonredundant components, with finer distinctions in each category.
