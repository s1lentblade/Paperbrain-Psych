## Abstract

Building designers’ decisions affect long term quality and life cycle cost of buildings. Designers’ decisions are usually latent in nature and hard to detect at the early stage of construction. This research looks at failure mechanisms that caused design-related latent defects and the design parameters that could prevent these defects. A 9-month building survey on 74 buildings found that the three most important design-related failure causes were weather impact, impacts from occupants, and loads and moisture from the wet areas.
