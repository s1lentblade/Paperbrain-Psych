# Risk and maintenance factors for eating pathology: A meta-analytic review.

**Authors:** Eric Stice
**Year:** 2002
**Citations:** 2,392
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.128.5.825

## Abstract

This meta-analytic review of prospective and experimental studies reveals that several accepted risk factors for eating pathology have not received empirical support (e.g., sexual abuse) or have received contradictory support (e.g.. dieting). There was consistent support for less-accepted risk factors(e.g., thin-ideal internalization) as well as emerging evidence for variables that potentiate and mitigate the effects of risk factors(e.g., social support) and factors that predict eating pathology maintenance(e.g., negative affect).
