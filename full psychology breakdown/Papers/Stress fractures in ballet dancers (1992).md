# Stress fractures in ballet dancers

**Authors:** Nancy J. Kadel, Richard A. Kronmal
**Year:** 1992
**Citations:** 223
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/036354659202000414

## Abstract

We surveyed 54 female dancers in two professional ballet companies. A total of 27 fractures were reported in 17 dancers. Metatarsal fractures were the most common (63%), followed by fractures of the tibia (22%) and spine (7%). Dancers who danced greater than 5 hours per day were significantly more likely to have a stress fracture than those dancing less than 5 hours per day. Dancers in the stress fracture group also had a significantly longer duration of amenorrhea than those in the group with no stress fractures.
