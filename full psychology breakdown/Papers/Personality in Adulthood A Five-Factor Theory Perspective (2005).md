**Authors:** Robert R. McCrae, Paul T. Costa
**Year:** 2005

## Abstract

"Now in a revised and expanded second edition, this book offers an up-to-date statement of current knowledge on personality and adult development. Presented are compelling updates and refinements to the argument advanced in the original volume - that personality traits remain remarkably stable after age 30, exerting a significant influence on individual adaptation and the life course. Describing the results of ongoing cross-sectional and longitudinal studies, Robert R. McCrae and Paul T. Costa, Jr., examine how traits can best be conceptualized and measured and trace their stability over time and across cultures.
