# The Impact of Technostress on Role Stress and Productivity

**Authors:** Monideepa Tarafdar, Qiang Tu, Bhanu S. Ragu-Nathan
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 1,586
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.2753/mis0742-1222240109

## Abstract

Based on empirical survey data, this paper uses concepts from sociotechnical theory and role theory to explore the effects of stress created by information and computer technology (ICT)—that is, "technostress"—on role stress and on individual productivity. We first explain different ways in which ICTs can create stress in users and identify factors that create technostress. We next propose three hypotheses: (1) technostress is inversely related to individual productivity, (2) role stress is inversely related to individual productivity, and (3) technostress is directly related to role stress. We then use structural equation modeling on survey data from ICT users in 223 organizations to test the hypotheses. The results show support for them.
