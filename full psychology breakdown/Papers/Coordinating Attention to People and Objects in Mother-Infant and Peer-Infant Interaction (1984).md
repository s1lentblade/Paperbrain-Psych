# Coordinating Attention to People and Objects in Mother-Infant and Peer-Infant Interaction

**Authors:** Roger Bakeman, Lauren B. Adamson
**Year:** 1984
**Citations:** 1,415

## Abstract

In a longitudinal study, infants 6-18 months of age were observed in their homes playing with their mothers and with peers. Of primary concern was how they coordinated their attention to people and objects. Observations were coded using a state-based scheme that included a state of coordinated joint engagement as well as states of person engagement, object engagement, onlooking, and passive joint engagement. All developmental trends observed were similar regardless of partner: person engagement declined with age, while coordinated joint engagement increased.
