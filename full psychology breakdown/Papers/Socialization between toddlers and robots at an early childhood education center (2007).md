# Socialization between toddlers and robots at an early childhood education center

**Authors:** Fumihide Tanaka, Javier R. Movellan
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 467
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0707769104

## Abstract

A state-of-the-art social robot was immersed in a classroom of toddlers for >5 months. The quality of the interaction between children and robots improved steadily for 27 sessions, quickly deteriorated for 15 sessions when the robot was reprogrammed to behave in a predictable manner, and improved in the last three sessions when the robot displayed again its full behavioral repertoire. Initially, the children treated the robot very differently than the way they treated each other. By the last sessions, 5 months later, they treated the robot as a peer rather than as a toy.
