# A Brief Measure for Assessing Generalized Anxiety Disorder

**Year:** 2006
**Citations:** 30,389

## Abstract

<h3>Background</h3> Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) is one of the most common mental disorders; however, there is no brief clinical measure for assessing GAD. The objective of this study was to develop a brief self-report scale to identify probable cases of GAD and evaluate its reliability and validity. <h3>Methods</h3> A criterion-standard study was performed in 15 primary care clinics in the United States from November 2004 through June 2005. Of a total of 2740 adult patients completing a study questionnaire, 965 patients had a telephone interview with a mental health professional within 1 week.
