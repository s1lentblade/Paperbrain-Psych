# Russian blues reveal effects of language on color discrimination

**Authors:** Jonathan Winawer, Lera Boroditsky
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 1,016
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.0701644104

## Abstract

English and Russian color terms divide the color spectrum differently. Unlike English, Russian makes an obligatory distinction between lighter blues ("goluboy") and darker blues ("siniy"). We investigated whether this linguistic difference leads to differences in color discrimination. We tested English and Russian speakers in a speeded color discrimination task using blue stimuli that spanned the siniy/goluboy border. We found that Russian speakers were faster to discriminate two colors when they fell into different linguistic categories in Russian (one siniy and the other goluboy) than when they were from the same linguistic category (both siniy or both goluboy).
