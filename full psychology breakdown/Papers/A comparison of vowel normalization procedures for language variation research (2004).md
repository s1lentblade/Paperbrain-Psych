# A comparison of vowel normalization procedures for language variation research

**Authors:** Patti Adank, Roeland van Hout
**Year:** 2004
**Citations:** 490
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1121/1.1795335

## Abstract

An evaluation of vowel normalization procedures for the purpose of studying language variation is presented. The procedures were compared on how effectively they (a) preserve phonemic information, (b) preserve information about the talker's regional background (or sociolinguistic information), and (c) minimize anatomical/physiological variation in acoustic representations of vowels. Recordings were made for 80 female talkers and 80 male talkers of Dutch. These talkers were stratified according to their gender and regional background. The normalization procedures were applied to measurements of the fundamental frequency and the first three formant frequencies for a large set of vowel tokens.
