# The Inventory of Depressive Symptomatology (IDS): psychometric properties

**Authors:** A. John Rush, Madhukar H. Trivedi
**Year:** 1996
**Citations:** 2,173
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1017/s0033291700035558

## Abstract

The psychometric properties of the 28- and 30-item versions of the Inventory of Depressive Symptomatology, Clinician-Rated (IDS-C) and Self-Report (IDS-SR) are reported in a total of 434 (28-item) and 337 (30-item) adult out-patients with current major depressive disorder and 118 adult euthymic subjects (15 remitted depressed and 103 normal controls). Cronbach's alpha ranged from 0.92 to 0.94 for the total sample and from 0.76 to 0.82 for those with current depression. Item total correlations, as well as several tests of concurrent and discriminant validity are reported.
