# Longitudinal evidence that psychopathy scores in early adolescence predict adult psychopathy.

**Authors:** Donald R. Lynam, Magda Stouthamer‐Loeber
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 552
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0021-843x.116.1.155

## Abstract

This study examined the relation between psychopathy assessed at age 13 by using the mother-reported Childhood Psychopathy Scale (D. R. Lynam, 1997) and psychopathy assessed at age 24 by using the interviewer-rated Psychopathy Checklist: Screening Version (PCL:SV; S. D. Hart, D. N. Cox, & R. D. Hare, 1995). Data from over 250 participants of the middle sample of the Pittsburgh Youth Study were used to examine this relation; approximately 9% of the sample met criteria for a possible PCL:SV diagnosis.
