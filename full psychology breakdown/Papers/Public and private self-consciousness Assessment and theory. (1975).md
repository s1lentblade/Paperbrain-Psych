# Public and private self-consciousness: Assessment and theory.

**Authors:** Allan Fenigstein, Arnold H. Buss
**Year:** 1975
**Citations:** 3,714
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/h0076760

## Abstract

Describes the development of a scale to assess individual differences in self-consciousness. Construction of the scale involved testing the 38 initial items with 130 female and 82 male undergraduates. A principal components factor analysis of the data yielded 3 factors accounting for 43% of the variance: Private Self-Consciousness, Public Self-Consciousness, and Social Anxiety. The final version of the scale, which contained 23 items, was administered to several groups of undergraduates (N = 668) to obtain norms, test-retest (2 wks), subscale correlation, and reliability data.
