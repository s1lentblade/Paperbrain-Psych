# Competence of Depressed Patients for Consent to Research

**Authors:** Paul S. Appelbaum, David J. Kupfer
**Year:** 1999
**Citations:** 278
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1176/ajp.156.9.1380

## Abstract

This outpatient group with major depression showed few impairments in their decision-making capacities related to research. As in other studies, some concerns were raised about subjects' appreciation that treatment assignments would not be individualized for their needs. Examination of hospitalized patients and those with psychotic depression would help to determine whether they show greater degrees of impairment. The MacCAT-CR was easily adapted for use with this depressed group.
