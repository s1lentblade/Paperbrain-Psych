# Readability of Text Scrolled on Visual Display Terminals as a Function of Window Size

**Authors:** Robert L. Duchnicky, Paul A. Kolers
**Year:** 1983
**Citations:** 222
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/001872088302500605

## Abstract

Readability of text scrolled on visual display terminals was studied as a function of three different line lengths, two different character densities, and five different window heights (either 1, 2, 3, 4, or 20 lines). All three variables significantly affected reading rate, but to markedly different extents. Lines of full and two-thirds screen width were read, on average, 25% faster than lines of one-third screen width. Text appearing at a density of 80 characters per line was read 30% faster than text in a format of 40 characters per line.
