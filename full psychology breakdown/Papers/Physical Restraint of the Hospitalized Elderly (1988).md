**Authors:** Neville E. Strumpf, Lois K. Evans
**Year:** 1988
**Citations:** 259

## Abstract

Twenty elderly restrained patients and their primary nurses (N = 18) were studied to determine (a) the subjective impact of physical restraints on the patient and (b) the nurses' beliefs about use of restraints. Despite reported cognitive impairments, patients vividly described anger, discomfort, resistance, and fear in response to the experience of physical restraint. The decision to restrain posed a conflict between protection of the patient and beliefs about professional behavior for the nursing staff.
