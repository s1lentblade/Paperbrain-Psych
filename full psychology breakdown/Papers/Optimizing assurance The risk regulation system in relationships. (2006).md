# Optimizing assurance: The risk regulation system in relationships.

**Authors:** Susan Murray, Nancy L. Collins
**Year:** 2006
**Citations:** 795
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.132.5.641

## Abstract

A model of risk regulation is proposed to explain how people balance the goal of seeking closeness to a romantic partner against the opposing goal of minimizing the likelihood and pain of rejection. The central premise is that confidence in a partner's positive regard and caring allows people to risk seeking dependence and connectedness. The risk regulation system consists of 3 interconnected "if--then" contingency rules, 1 cognitive, 1 affective, and 1 behavioral. The authors describe how general perceptions of a partner's regard structure the sensitivity of these 3 "if--then" rules in risky relationship situations. The authors then describe the consequences of such situated "if--then" rule
