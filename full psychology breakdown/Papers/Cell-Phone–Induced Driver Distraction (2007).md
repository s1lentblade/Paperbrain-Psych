# Cell-Phone–Induced Driver Distraction

**Authors:** David L. Strayer, Frank A. Drews
**Year:** 2007
**Citations:** 391

## Abstract

Our research examined the effects of hands-free cell-phone conversations on simulated driving. We found that even when participants looked directly at objects in the driving environment, they were less likely to create a durable memory of those objects if they were conversing on a cell phone. This pattern was obtained for objects of both high and low relevance, suggesting that very little semantic analysis of the objects occurs outside the restricted focus of attention.
