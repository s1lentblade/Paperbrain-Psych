# Role of self-efficacy and self-concept beliefs in mathematical problem solving: A path analysis.

**Authors:** Frank Pajares, M. David Miller
**Year:** 1994
**Citations:** 1,731
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-0663.86.2.193

## Abstract

Path analysis was used to test the predictive and mediational role of self-efficacy beliefs in mathematical problem solving. Results revealed that math self-efficacy was more predictive of problem solving than was math self-concept, perceived usefulness of mathematics, prior experi-ence with mathematics, or gender (N = 350). Self-efficacy also mediated the effect of gender and prior experience on self-concept, perceived usefulness, and problem solving. Gender and prior experience influenced self-concept, perceived usefulness, and problem solving largely through the mediational role of self-efficacy.
