# Persuasion and Healing: A Comparative Study of Psychotherapy

**Authors:** Jerome D. Frank
**Year:** 1961
**Citations:** 1,399

## Abstract

This popular study of psychological healingtreats topics ranging from religious revivalism and magical to contemporary psychotherapies, from the role of the shaman in nonindustrialized societies to the traditional mental hospital. Jerome and Julia Frank (who are father and daughter) contend that these therapies share common elements that improve the moraleof sufferers. And in combating the demoralizing meaningthat people attach to their experiences, the authors argue, many therapies are surprisingly similar to rhetoric (the art of persuasion) and to hermeneutics (the study of meanings).
