# Children's perceptions of the personal relationships in their social networks.

**Authors:** Wyndol Furman, Duane Buhrmester
**Year:** 1985
**Citations:** 2,123
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0012-1649.21.6.1016

## Abstract

Relatively few investigators have compared and contrasted the characteristics of different kinds of relationships in children&amp;apos;s social networks. In the present study, 199 fifth- and sixth-grade children completed Network of Relationships Inventories, which assessed 10 qualities of their relationships with mothers, fathers, siblings, grandparents, friends, and teachers. Consistent with Weiss&amp;apos;s (1974) theory, children reported seeking different provisions from different individuals.
