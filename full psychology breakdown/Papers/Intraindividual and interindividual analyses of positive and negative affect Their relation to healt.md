# Intraindividual and interindividual analyses of positive and negative affect: Their relation to health complaints, perceived stress, and daily activities.

**Authors:** David Watson
**Year:** 1988
**Citations:** 1,014
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.54.6.1020

## Abstract

I examined correlates of Negative Affect (NA) and Positive Affect (PA) through both within- and between-subjects analyses. Eighty subjects completed a daily questionnaire for 6-8 weeks. Each day they rated (a) their mood, (b) the extent to which they suffered from various minor physical problems, (c) their level of stress, (d) the time they spent socializing, and (e) whether or not they had exercised. Subjects also completed several trait tests measuring their general affective level, frequency of health problems, and social tendencies.
