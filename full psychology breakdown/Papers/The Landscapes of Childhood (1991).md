## Abstract

This article deals with children's relation to the environment from actual and retrospective points of view, delineated by empirical research and by theoretical analysis. The empirical research investigated the environmental preferences and the nature of the experiences of being outdoors as reflected in adult's ( N = 198) recollections and in children's ( N = 174) actual approach. Among the findings was that almost all adults identify the most significant place in their childhood with the outdoors.
